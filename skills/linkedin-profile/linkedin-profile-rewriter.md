# LinkedIn Profile Rewriter

## Purpose

This skill takes the audit results and rewrites every key section of the user's LinkedIn profile. The output is complete, copy-paste-ready text for each section — optimized to convert profile visitors from the user's ICP into conversations.

This is the most hands-on skill in the LinkedIn Profile phase. The user should walk away with a fully rewritten profile they can update immediately.

---

## Prerequisites

Before running this rewriter, you must have outputs from:

1. **LinkedIn Profile Audit** — To know what is broken and what the priority fixes are.
2. **ICP Architect** — To know the exact buyer: title, industry, pain points, desires, and the language they use.
3. **Offer Positioning Engine** — To know the core offer, mechanism, transformation, proof points, and differentiators.

---

## Information Gathering

In addition to the data from prerequisite skills, ask the user:

1. **What is the single most impressive result you have delivered for a client?** (Include numbers, timeline, and context.)
2. **What is your origin story — why did you start doing this work?** (A 2-3 sentence version is fine. This fuels the About section.)
3. **What are the top 3 objections your prospects have before buying?** (These get addressed in the profile copy.)
4. **What is your preferred CTA?** (Book a call, DM a keyword, download a resource, visit a website.)
5. **What credibility markers do you want to highlight?** (Years of experience, number of clients, revenue generated, notable logos, certifications, awards, press.)
6. **What are 3-5 keywords your ICP would search on LinkedIn to find someone like you?** (These get woven into the headline, about, and experience sections for search discoverability.)
7. **Do you have any existing assets to feature?** (Lead magnets, case studies, podcast episodes, articles, video testimonials.)

## Section 1: Headline Rewrite

### The Rules

- You have 220 characters. Use all of them.
- The headline appears in search results, connection requests, comments, and DMs. It is the most visible piece of text on your profile.
- It must answer: Who do you help? What result do you deliver?
- It must include at least one keyword your ICP would search for.
- Use pipe characters (|) to separate distinct elements.

### The 5 Headline Formulas

**Formula 1: The Helper**
Structure: "I help [ICP] [achieve result] through [mechanism]"
Best for: Service providers, consultants, agencies
Example: "I help B2B SaaS companies reduce churn by 40% through data-driven retention systems"

**Formula 2: The Scale Marker**
Structure: "Helping [number]+ [ICP] [transformation] | [credibility marker]"
Best for: Coaches, consultants with a track record
Example: "Helping 300+ agency owners scale past $50K/month | From $0 to $1.2M in my own agency in 18 months"

**Formula 3: The Result Lead**
Structure: "[Specific result] for [ICP] | [mechanism or differentiator] | [CTA or credibility]"
Best for: Anyone with strong quantifiable results
Example: "$47M+ in pipeline generated for B2B tech companies | Outbound systems that book 30+ meetings/month | Free audit below"

**Formula 4: The Curiosity Lead**
Structure: "[Contrarian or surprising statement] | [what you actually do] | [for whom]"
Best for: Thought leaders, those with a unique methodology
Example: "Cold email is not dead — your strategy is | Building outbound engines for B2B teams doing $5M-$50M | 82% open rates"

**Formula 5: The Authority Lead**
Structure: "[Title/Role] | [Key achievement] | [Who you serve]"
Best for: Executives, founders with brand authority
Example: "Founder of [Company] | Grew 3 SaaS products to $10M+ ARR | Now helping early-stage founders do the same"

### Character Limit Strategy

Your 220 characters should be allocated roughly as follows:

- **Segment 1 (80-100 chars):** Primary value proposition — who you help and what result you deliver
- **Segment 2 (50-70 chars):** Credibility marker or differentiator — why you specifically
- **Segment 3 (40-60 chars):** CTA or keyword — what to do next or a search-friendly term

Use `|` to separate segments. Do not use emojis in the headline — they reduce perceived professionalism in B2B contexts.

### Keyword Strategy for Headlines

LinkedIn's search algorithm heavily weights the headline. Include 1-2 terms your ICP would actually type into the LinkedIn search bar:

- Think job-function terms, not marketing buzzwords: "outbound sales," "lead generation," "tax planning," "managed IT," "executive coaching"
- Test by searching LinkedIn yourself — what do you want to appear for?
- Place keywords naturally within value proposition statements, not as a keyword-stuffed list at the end

## Section 2: About Section Rewrite

### The "Story-Sell" Framework

The About section has 2,600 characters. Every line has a job. Here is the framework:

#### Line-by-Line Breakdown

**Lines 1-2: THE HOOK (visible before "see more")**
Job: Stop the scroll. Make the reader click "see more."
Strategy: Lead with a bold claim, a pain point the ICP feels viscerally, or a surprising data point.

Formulas:
- "Most [ICPs] are [doing something wrong]. Here is what [top performers] do instead."
- "[Specific painful stat about the ICP's world]."
- "If you are a [ICP] struggling with [specific problem], read this."
- "In [time period], we helped [number] [ICPs] achieve [result]. Here is how."
- "[Contrarian take that challenges a common belief in the ICP's industry]."

**Lines 3-6: THE PROBLEM**
Job: Show the reader you deeply understand their world.
Strategy: Describe the specific problems they face. Use their language. Name the symptoms they experience daily.

Template:
> You have tried [common solution #1]. You have tried [common solution #2]. Maybe you have even [common solution #3].
>
> But you are still [core frustration]. Still [secondary frustration]. Still [emotional frustration].

**Lines 7-9: THE AGITATION**
Job: Raise the stakes. Make the problem feel urgent.
Strategy: Show what happens if they do not solve this. Quantify the cost of inaction.

Template:
> Every month this goes unsolved, you are leaving [$ amount] on the table. Your [competitors/peers] are [doing the thing you help with], and the gap is only growing.

**Lines 10-14: THE SOLUTION**
Job: Position your approach as the answer — without being salesy.
Strategy: Describe your methodology or approach at a high level. Focus on the "what" and "why," not the "how" (save the how for the sales call).

Template:
> That is why I built [your methodology/approach]. It is a [brief description] that helps [ICPs] go from [current state] to [desired state] in [timeframe].
>
> Instead of [old way], we [new way]. Instead of [old approach], we [new approach].

**Lines 15-18: THE PROOF**
Job: Eliminate skepticism. Show it works.
Strategy: Stack 3-5 proof points. Use specific numbers. Name results, not activities.

Template:
> Here is what that looks like in practice:
>
> - [Client type] went from [before] to [after] in [timeframe]
> - [Number]+ [ICPs] have used this to [achieve result]
> - Average client sees [specific metric improvement] within [timeframe]

**Lines 19-21: THE CTA**
Job: Tell them exactly what to do next.
Strategy: One CTA. Make it specific and low friction. Offer value, not a commitment.

Templates:
> Want to see if this could work for your [company/team]? DM me the word "[keyword]" and I will send you [specific resource/offer].

> If you are a [ICP] doing [qualifier], I would love to show you how this works. Book a free [X]-minute call here: [link]

> Grab the free [resource name] — it walks you through [what they will learn]. Link in my Featured section.

### Formatting Rules

- Use blank lines between paragraphs (LinkedIn compresses formatting — add extra line breaks)
- Keep paragraphs to 2-3 lines maximum
- Use bullet points sparingly — they work for proof lists but not for narrative sections
- Do not use hashtags in the About section — they look spammy and are not effective here
- Avoid all-caps words (reads as shouting)
- Do not use emojis as bullet points or decorators — keep it professional for B2B

### 3 Complete About Section Templates

**Template A: The Pain-Solution Expert**
Best for: Consultants, agencies, B2B service providers

```
Most [ICPs] waste [time/money] on [common mistake] — and wonder why [desired result] stays out of reach.

I have spent [X years] working with [number]+ [ICPs] to fix this.

Here is the pattern I see:

You are [symptom #1]. You are [symptom #2]. And every time you try [common but ineffective approach], it [why it fails].

The real problem is not [surface problem]. It is [root cause].

That is exactly what [your methodology name] solves.

It is a [brief description of approach] built specifically for [ICPs] who [qualifier — size, stage, situation].

What it looks like in practice:

- [Client type] went from [before metric] to [after metric] in [timeframe]
- [Client type] achieved [result] after struggling with [problem] for [time]
- On average, clients see [X% improvement] in [metric] within [timeframe]

If you are a [ICP] ready to [desired transformation], here is your next step:

[Single clear CTA with specific instructions]
```

**Template B: The Origin Story Seller**
Best for: Founders, coaches, anyone with a compelling personal journey

```
[X years] ago, I was [relatable starting point that mirrors ICP's current situation].

I [tried common approaches]. I [experienced the same frustrations your ICP has].

Then I [discovered/built/figured out the thing that changed everything].

In the [time period] since, [your most impressive result — stated in numbers].

Now I help [ICPs] do the same thing — without [pain point #1], without [pain point #2], and without [pain point #3].

How it works:

[2-3 sentences describing your methodology at a high level]

The results speak for themselves:

- [Result #1 with specifics]
- [Result #2 with specifics]
- [Result #3 with specifics]

I work with [qualifier: type of client, size, stage, situation].

[CTA: specific next step]
```

**Template C: The Authority Educator**
Best for: Thought leaders, subject matter experts, those known for a specific framework

```
[Bold, counterintuitive statement about the ICP's industry or common approach].

[Follow-up that explains why the statement is true — 2-3 lines].

I have spent [X years] studying [domain]. I have worked with [number] [ICPs] across [industries/contexts]. And I keep seeing the same [number] mistakes:

1. [Mistake #1 — briefly described]
2. [Mistake #2 — briefly described]
3. [Mistake #3 — briefly described]

When you fix these, everything changes. [Describe the transformation in 1-2 lines.]

That is what [your framework/methodology] is built to do. It gives [ICPs] a [clear, simple description] to go from [current state] to [desired state].

Proof it works:

- [Result with specifics]
- [Result with specifics]
- [Result with specifics]

If you want to see how this applies to your [business/team/company]:

[CTA]
```

### Keyword Weaving Strategy

Identify 3-5 keywords from the ICP research and naturally incorporate them:
- In the problem description (use the terms your ICP uses to describe their challenges)
- In the solution description (use industry-standard terms for your service category)
- In the proof section (mention specific industries, company types, or metrics)

Do NOT keyword-stuff. Every keyword should fit naturally within a sentence that would read well without it.

## Section 3: Experience Section Rewrite

### Framework: Each Role as a Mini Case Study

For the current role, use this structure:

**Company Description (the company tagline field):**
- Write this as a benefit statement, not a factual description
- Bad: "[Company] is a digital marketing agency founded in 2019."
- Good: "[Company] helps B2B companies generate qualified pipeline through outbound systems that actually convert."

**Role Description (the main text area):**

Use the Context > Challenge > Action > Result framework:

```
[One sentence: what the company does and who it serves]

[The challenge/gap in the market that you are addressing]

What we deliver:
- [Result/outcome #1 with specifics]
- [Result/outcome #2 with specifics]
- [Result/outcome #3 with specifics]

[Optional: Notable clients, industries served, or scale of work — if it adds credibility]

[Optional: CTA or link to learn more]
```

**Bullet Point Formulas for Achievements:**

- "[Action verb] [what you did] resulting in [quantified outcome] for [who]"
  Example: "Built an outbound system generating 45+ qualified meetings per month for B2B SaaS clients"

- "Helped [number] [client type] achieve [specific result] in [timeframe]"
  Example: "Helped 200+ e-commerce brands increase email revenue by an average of 32% within 60 days"

- "Grew [metric] from [before] to [after] in [timeframe] through [mechanism]"
  Example: "Grew client pipeline from $200K to $2.1M in quarterly revenue within 6 months through a combination of cold email and LinkedIn outreach"

### Past Roles Strategy

Past roles should support a narrative. They answer the question: "How did this person become an expert in this?"

- Keep descriptions shorter than the current role (2-3 bullets per role)
- Focus on transferable skills and results that build credibility for the current offer
- If a past role is unrelated to your current positioning, either remove it, minimize it (title only), or reframe it to highlight transferable skills
- If you have 10+ years of experience, only detail the last 3-4 roles

## Section 4: Featured Section Setup

### The Conversion Path Logic

Your Featured section items should follow a deliberate order. Think of it as a funnel:

**Slot 1 (leftmost, most visible): Primary CTA**
This is your most important conversion asset. Options:
- Booking link for a free consultation/audit/strategy call
- Lead magnet download (guide, template, checklist, assessment)
- Top-performing post that generated the most inbound conversations

**Slot 2: Social Proof**
Back up your positioning with evidence:
- A case study post (detailed client result story)
- A video testimonial or compilation
- A carousel showing before/after results

**Slot 3: Expertise Demonstration**
Show you know what you are talking about:
- A high-engagement educational post
- An article or newsletter link
- A podcast episode or interview where you are the guest

**Slot 4-5 (optional): Supporting Assets**
Additional conversion or credibility content:
- A second lead magnet or resource
- A top post from the last 30 days
- A company overview or product demo video

### Featured Section Optimization Tips

- **Custom thumbnails matter.** If you link to an external URL, LinkedIn will pull the page's Open Graph image. Make sure it looks good. If it does not, use a LinkedIn article or post instead.
- **Titles are editable.** For LinkedIn posts that appear in Featured, you cannot edit the title. For external links, write a compelling title (e.g., "Free: The 5-Step Playbook to 3x Your Outbound Pipeline" not "Blog Post").
- **Review quarterly.** Replace outdated content. Feature your best-performing recent post. Keep the CTA link current.
- **Test the links.** Click every link from both desktop and mobile. Broken links destroy credibility.

## Section 5: Skills & Endorsements

### Skill Selection Strategy

1. **List the top 3 skills that your ICP would search for on LinkedIn.** These become your pinned skills.
   - Think like your buyer: if they were looking for someone who does what you do, what would they type?
   - Use LinkedIn's search bar to test — start typing and see what autocomplete suggests
   - Examples: "Lead Generation," "B2B Sales," "Email Marketing," "Tax Planning," "Managed IT Services"

2. **Add 5-10 supporting skills** that reinforce your positioning.
   - These should be specific and relevant: "CRM Implementation," "Sales Funnel Optimization," "Financial Modeling"
   - Avoid generic filler: "Leadership," "Communication," "Microsoft Office"

3. **Remove skills that dilute your positioning.**
   - If you used to be a graphic designer but now sell marketing strategy, remove "Adobe Photoshop"
   - If you have skills from a previous career, remove them unless they support your current offer

### Endorsement Acquisition Strategy

- Endorse 20-30 people in your network for relevant skills. Many will reciprocate.
- When asking for endorsements directly, be specific: "Would you mind endorsing me for [specific skill]? It helps with LinkedIn search visibility."
- Focus endorsement requests on your top 3 pinned skills first.
- Ask clients to endorse you after a successful engagement — timing matters.

## Section 6: Profile Photo & Banner Recommendations

### Profile Photo Guidelines

If the audit flagged the photo, provide these specific recommendations:

- Invest in a professional headshot ($100-300 for a session — it pays for itself many times over)
- If budget is tight: use natural lighting (face a window), plain wall background, phone camera on portrait mode
- Dress one level above your ICP's expectations (if they wear casual, you wear business casual)
- Test the photo: show it to 3 people and ask "What is this person's profession?" — their answer should match your positioning
- Use a tool like Photofeeler to get objective ratings on your photo (competence, likability, influence)

### Banner Image Guidelines

Provide recommendations based on the audit:

- **Option A: Value Proposition Banner** — Your headline restated visually with a supporting image. Include a CTA or credibility marker. Best for: most B2B professionals.
- **Option B: Social Proof Banner** — Client logos, media logos, or result metrics displayed visually. Best for: those with recognizable clients or impressive numbers.
- **Option C: Personal Brand Banner** — Your face alongside your tagline. Best for: coaches, consultants, personal brands.
- **Design tools:** Canva (free templates available at 1584x396 resolution), Figma, or hire a designer on Fiverr ($20-50).
- **Always test on mobile.** The banner crops differently. Make sure text and key elements are visible.

## How to Test the New Profile

After the user implements the rewrite, provide these measurement benchmarks:

### Week 1 Metrics to Track
- **Profile views:** Check the "Who's viewed your profile" section. Note the baseline number.
- **Search appearances:** LinkedIn shows how many times you appeared in search. Note the baseline.
- **Connection request acceptance rate:** If you are sending outbound requests, track the percentage that accept. Baseline this.

### Week 2-4 Comparison
- Profile views should increase 30-100% if the rewrite is well-targeted
- Search appearances should increase as new keywords take effect (LinkedIn indexes changes within 48-72 hours)
- Connection acceptance rate should improve 10-25% with a stronger headline and profile

### What to Look For in "Who Viewed Your Profile"
- Are the viewers matching your ICP? (Right titles, right industries, right company sizes)
- If viewers are wrong audience: headline keywords or positioning may be attracting the wrong people — adjust
- If view count drops: you may have removed a high-traffic keyword — check what changed

### Ongoing Optimization
- Revisit the headline quarterly — test new formulas
- Update the About section whenever you have a new proof point or case study
- Rotate Featured section content monthly to keep it fresh
- Track which version of the profile correlates with the highest inbound message rate
