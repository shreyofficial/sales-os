# LinkedIn Profile Audit

## Purpose

Your LinkedIn profile is not a resume. It is a landing page. Every day, potential buyers visit your profile before deciding whether to accept your connection request, reply to your DM, or take your meeting. This audit scores every section of your profile against what actually converts — not vanity metrics, not "best practices" from 2018, but what makes a prospect think "I need to talk to this person."

This skill audits the user's LinkedIn profile section by section, scores it, diagnoses what is broken, and produces a ranked list of fixes that will have the highest impact on converting profile visitors into conversations.

---

## Prerequisites

Before running this audit, ensure you have completed:

1. **Business Context Compiler** — You need to know what the business does, how it makes money, and what makes it different.
2. **ICP Architect** — You need to know exactly who the ideal buyer is: their title, industry, pain points, and language.
3. **Offer Positioning Engine** — You need to know the core offer, the transformation it delivers, and the proof behind it.

Without these, the audit will default to generic advice. With them, every recommendation is calibrated to attract the right buyer.

---

## Information Gathering

Ask the user for the following:

1. **LinkedIn Profile URL** — so you can reference their actual profile.
2. **Current headline** — copy-paste the full text.
3. **Current About section** — copy-paste the full text.
4. **Current Experience section** — at minimum, the current role description.
5. **Featured section contents** — what is pinned, if anything.
6. **Number of recommendations** — and a summary of what they say.
7. **Recent activity** — how often they post, what kind of content, typical engagement.
8. **Profile photo description** — professional headshot, casual, logo, etc.
9. **Banner image description** — what it shows, if anything custom.
10. **Current follower/connection count** — rough number is fine.

If the user cannot provide all of these, work with what is available. Flag missing information in the audit output.

## The Three Core Tests

Before scoring individual sections, run these three diagnostic tests. These determine whether the profile works as a whole.

### Test 1: The 3-Second Test

When your ICP lands on your profile, they see three things above the fold: your photo, your headline, and the first line of your about section (on desktop) or your headline alone (on mobile). In 3 seconds, they must be able to answer:

| Question | What to look for |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Who do you help?** | Is the ICP explicitly named? (e.g., "B2B SaaS founders" not "businesses") |
| **What transformation do you deliver?** | Is there a concrete outcome? (e.g., "book 30+ qualified meetings/month" not "grow your business") |
| **Why should they care?** | Is there a credibility signal or differentiator? (e.g., "helped 200+ companies" or "without paid ads") |

**Scoring the 3-Second Test:**
- All 3 clear = PASS
- 2 of 3 clear = PARTIAL (profile will convert some visitors but lose many)
- 1 or 0 clear = FAIL (profile is actively losing you opportunities)

### Test 2: The Credibility Scan

Scroll the entire profile. Count every piece of proof:

- Specific numbers or metrics mentioned (revenue generated, clients served, results achieved)
- Named companies or logos (in experience, featured, or banner)
- Recommendations from relevant people (clients > colleagues > random connections)
- Content that demonstrates expertise (posts with real engagement, not just motivational quotes)
- External validation (awards, press mentions, speaking engagements, certifications that matter)

**Scoring the Credibility Scan:**
- 10+ proof points spread across multiple sections = STRONG
- 5-9 proof points = MODERATE (enough for warm leads, not enough for cold outreach)
- 0-4 proof points = WEAK (profile relies on claims, not evidence — prospects will be skeptical)

### Test 3: The CTA Clarity Check

A prospect just read your entire profile and is interested. What do they do next?

- Is there a clear call to action in the About section? (e.g., "DM me 'growth' to get started" or "Book a free strategy call: [link]")
- Is there a CTA in the Featured section? (e.g., a link to book a call, download a resource, or visit a landing page)
- Is the CTA specific and low-friction? ("Book a free 15-minute call" > "Let's connect" > nothing)
- Is there only ONE primary CTA? (Multiple CTAs = no CTA — the paradox of choice kills conversion)

**Scoring the CTA Clarity Check:**
- Clear, single, low-friction CTA present in 2+ places = PASS
- CTA exists but is vague or buried = PARTIAL
- No clear CTA anywhere = FAIL

## Section-by-Section Scoring

Score each section on a 1-10 scale using the criteria below. A score of 7+ means the section is working for you. A score of 4-6 means it is neutral (not helping, not hurting). A score of 1-3 means it is actively damaging your conversion rate.

---

### 1. Profile Photo (Weight: HIGH)

**What excellence looks like:**
- Professional headshot with a clean, non-distracting background
- Face takes up 60-70% of the frame
- Good lighting — no harsh shadows, no backlight
- Friendly but confident expression (slight smile, direct eye contact)
- Dressed appropriately for the industry (match what your ICP expects)
- High resolution, not pixelated or cropped from a group photo
- Recent — looks like you, not you from 10 years ago

**Common mistakes:**
- Using a company logo instead of a face (kills trust immediately)
- Casual selfie, vacation photo, or group photo cropped awkwardly
- Sunglasses, hat, or anything obscuring the face
- Dark, blurry, or low-resolution image
- Photo that does not match the tone of the business (e.g., a suit-and-tie photo for a creative agency)
- No photo at all (profiles without photos get 9x fewer connection requests)

**Scoring criteria:**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 9-10 | Professional headshot, great lighting, confident expression, matches industry tone |
| 7-8 | Good quality photo, clear face, but could be more polished or intentional |
| 5-6 | Decent photo but clearly not professional — phone quality, distracting background |
| 3-4 | Low quality, poorly lit, or inappropriate for business context |
| 1-2 | No photo, logo instead of face, or unrecognizable image |

---

### 2. Banner Image (Weight: MEDIUM)

**What excellence looks like:**
- Custom designed banner that communicates your value proposition visually
- Includes one or more of: a tagline, your core offer, a credibility marker (logos, metrics), a CTA
- Clean design with readable text — works on both desktop and mobile
- Brand-consistent colors and fonts
- Dimensions: 1584 x 396 pixels (LinkedIn's recommended size)
- Does not repeat what the headline already says — adds new information

**Common mistakes:**
- Default LinkedIn blue gradient (signals "I do not care about my profile")
- Cluttered design with too much text or too many elements
- Text that is cut off on mobile (always test mobile rendering)
- Stock photo with no business relevance
- Low resolution or pixelated image
- Banner that contradicts or confuses the positioning in the headline

**Scoring criteria:**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 9-10 | Custom banner with clear value prop, credibility, and/or CTA. Looks professional. |
| 7-8 | Custom banner with some value, but missing a key element (CTA, proof, or clarity) |
| 5-6 | Generic but not harmful — a stock photo or simple design with no messaging |
| 3-4 | Default LinkedIn banner or a low-quality custom image |
| 1-2 | Banner that actively confuses or detracts (wrong branding, pixelated, irrelevant) |

---

### 3. Headline (Weight: CRITICAL)

The headline is the single most important line of text on your profile. It appears everywhere: in search results, connection requests, comments, DMs, and on the profile itself. It is also your primary search ranking factor on LinkedIn.

**What excellence looks like:**
- Clearly states who you help and the result you deliver
- Uses language your ICP would actually use (not industry jargon they do not know)
- Includes relevant keywords for LinkedIn search discoverability
- Uses the full 220-character limit strategically
- Contains a credibility marker or differentiator
- Pipe characters (|) or bullet points to separate distinct elements
- Reads naturally — not keyword-stuffed

**Common mistakes:**
- Job title only: "CEO at Company Name" (tells prospects nothing about value)
- Vague buzzwords: "Helping businesses grow" or "Passionate about innovation"
- Self-focused: "Award-winning marketer" (your ICP does not care about your awards — they care about their problems)
- Too clever: Puns, jokes, or cryptic statements that require explanation
- Wasting characters on things that appear elsewhere (like company name, which is shown separately)
- No keywords: Great headline that no one ever finds in search

**Scoring criteria:**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 9-10 | ICP identified, transformation clear, credibility signal present, keywords optimized, full character usage |
| 7-8 | Strong but missing one element (e.g., no keywords, or no credibility signal) |
| 5-6 | Communicates what you do but not the transformation or who you do it for |
| 3-4 | Generic title + company name with no positioning |
| 1-2 | Empty, misleading, or a headline that actively repels the ICP |

---

### 4. About Section (Weight: CRITICAL)

The About section is your sales page. It is the only place on your profile where you have 2,600 characters of uninterrupted space to persuade a prospect. The first 2-3 lines appear before the "see more" fold — these must hook the reader into clicking.

**What excellence looks like:**
- First 2 lines are a compelling hook that creates curiosity or identifies the reader's pain
- Follows a clear narrative arc: Hook > Problem > Agitation > Solution > Proof > CTA
- Written in first person — it is a conversation, not a bio
- Includes specific results, numbers, and proof points
- Addresses the ICP directly ("If you are a [ICP] struggling with [problem]...")
- Ends with a clear, single call to action
- Formatted for readability: short paragraphs, line breaks, occasional bullets
- Naturally includes keywords relevant to your service and ICP

**Common mistakes:**
- Starting with "I am a passionate..." (no one clicks "see more" for this)
- Writing in third person ("John is a seasoned professional...") — feels impersonal
- Wall of text with no line breaks or formatting
- Listing every service, skill, and certification (feature-dumping)
- No CTA — the reader finishes and has nowhere to go
- Multiple CTAs — confusing, reduces action
- Pure autobiography with no relevance to the reader's problems
- Generic superlatives: "results-driven," "thought leader," "strategic thinker"

**Scoring criteria:**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 9-10 | Hook is irresistible, story arc is clear, proof is specific, CTA is actionable, formatting is clean |
| 7-8 | Good structure and content but missing one key element (weak hook, no proof, or vague CTA) |
| 5-6 | Contains useful information but reads like a bio, not a sales page |
| 3-4 | Wall of text, unfocused, or talks entirely about the person with no ICP relevance |
| 1-2 | Empty, copied from a resume, or actively off-putting |

---

### 5. Featured Section (Weight: HIGH)

The Featured section sits right below the About section. It is visual, clickable, and most profiles leave it empty — which means yours stands out when it is used well. Think of it as your "evidence shelf."

**What excellence looks like:**
- 3-5 items pinned in a deliberate order (conversion path logic)
- First item is the primary CTA (lead magnet, booking link, or top-performing post)
- Mix of content types: a resource, a case study/result, a high-engagement post
- Each item has a compelling title and thumbnail
- Items are current and relevant (not from 2 years ago)
- Links work and lead to pages that reinforce your positioning

**Common mistakes:**
- Empty featured section (a missed opportunity)
- Too many items (7+ becomes overwhelming and nothing stands out)
- Items with no context — just a link with a default thumbnail
- Outdated content that no longer reflects your current offer
- All items are the same type (e.g., 5 blog posts — no variety)
- Items that contradict your positioning (e.g., featuring content about a past career)

**Scoring criteria:**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 9-10 | 3-5 items in logical order, mix of types, clear thumbnails, current content, primary CTA first |
| 7-8 | Good selection but missing optimization (poor thumbnails, suboptimal order, or one weak item) |
| 5-6 | Has items but they are random — no conversion logic behind the selection |
| 3-4 | 1-2 items that do not serve a clear purpose |
| 1-2 | Empty or features irrelevant/outdated content |

---

### 6. Experience Section (Weight: MEDIUM)

Most people treat this like a resume. For a sales-optimized profile, each role should read like a mini case study that reinforces why the ICP should trust you.

**What excellence looks like:**
- Current role clearly describes the transformation you deliver (not your responsibilities)
- Bullet points focus on outcomes: metrics, client results, scale of impact
- Company descriptions are benefit-focused (what the company does for its clients)
- Past roles build a narrative of progressive credibility in your space
- Each role entry has a rich media attachment where relevant (case studies, projects, articles)
- Keywords naturally woven into descriptions

**Common mistakes:**
- Copy-pasting from a resume ("Responsible for managing a team of...")
- No description at all — just title and company
- Task-focused instead of result-focused
- Inconsistent dates or gaps that raise questions without explanation
- Including irrelevant roles that dilute your positioning (e.g., a college retail job when you are a B2B consultant)

**Scoring criteria:**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 9-10 | Current role is a sales page, past roles build credibility, specific results throughout |
| 7-8 | Good current role description but past roles are thin or resume-like |
| 5-6 | Basic descriptions present but reads like a resume, not a sales tool |
| 3-4 | Minimal descriptions, mostly titles and dates |
| 1-2 | Empty, irrelevant roles only, or actively confusing career narrative |

---

### 7. Skills & Endorsements (Weight: LOW-MEDIUM)

Skills influence LinkedIn search results. Endorsements provide a lightweight form of social proof. Neither will make or break a profile, but they contribute to discoverability and credibility.

**What excellence looks like:**
- Top 3 pinned skills align exactly with what the ICP would search for
- 50+ endorsements on the top 3 skills
- Skills list reflects current positioning (not skills from a previous career)
- No irrelevant or contradictory skills (e.g., "Microsoft Excel" when you sell marketing strategy)

**Common mistakes:**
- Default skills that LinkedIn auto-suggested and were never curated
- Top skills are generic (e.g., "Leadership," "Communication") instead of specific to your domain
- Very few endorsements — signals low engagement or new profile
- Skills from a previous career that dilute current positioning

**Scoring criteria:**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 9-10 | Top 3 skills are ICP-search-aligned, 50+ endorsements each, list is curated |
| 7-8 | Good skill selection but low endorsement counts or 1-2 misaligned skills |
| 5-6 | Default or generic skills with moderate endorsements |
| 3-4 | Misaligned skills or very few endorsements |
| 1-2 | No skills listed or skills that contradict current positioning |

---

### 8. Recommendations (Weight: HIGH)

Recommendations are the most powerful trust signal on LinkedIn. They are public testimonials from real, identifiable people — which makes them more credible than anonymous case studies.

**What excellence looks like:**
- 5+ recommendations visible on the profile
- At least 2-3 are from clients or customers (not just colleagues or managers)
- Recommendations describe specific results or transformations (not just "great to work with")
- Recommenders match or resemble the ICP (social proof from peers is more persuasive)
- Most recent recommendation is within the last 12 months
- Recommendations are on the most relevant role (current position)

**Common mistakes:**
- Zero recommendations (a major credibility gap for sales profiles)
- All recommendations are from colleagues at the same company (signals no client relationships)
- Vague, generic recommendations ("John is a hard worker and great team player")
- Recommendations only on old roles that are no longer relevant
- Not reciprocating — which reduces willingness of others to write for you

**Scoring criteria:**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 9-10 | 5+ client/customer recommendations with specific results, recent, on current role |
| 7-8 | 3-4 good recommendations but missing client perspective or specificity |
| 5-6 | 1-2 recommendations, generic in nature |
| 3-4 | Recommendations only from colleagues, or only on old roles |
| 1-2 | Zero recommendations |

---

### 9. Activity & Content (Weight: HIGH)

Your recent activity is visible on your profile. If a prospect scrolls down and sees that you post regularly about topics relevant to their problems, it massively increases trust. If they see you have not posted in 6 months, or that you only share company announcements, it is a negative signal.

**What excellence looks like:**
- Posts 3-5 times per week consistently
- Content is relevant to the ICP's pain points and interests
- Posts get engagement (comments, not just likes) from people who look like the ICP
- Mix of content types: text posts, carousels, videos, articles
- Content demonstrates expertise through insights, not just opinions
- Engages with others' content in a thoughtful way (comments on ICP's posts)

**Common mistakes:**
- No posting activity at all (or last post was months ago)
- Only sharing company content or press releases
- Posting about personal topics that are irrelevant to the ICP
- High frequency but low quality — posting for the sake of posting
- Only engaging with content from peers, not from ICPs
- Content that attracts other service providers, not buyers

**Scoring criteria:**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 9-10 | Consistent posting (3-5x/week), ICP-relevant content, strong engagement from target audience |
| 7-8 | Regular posting with good content but inconsistent cadence or engagement from wrong audience |
| 5-6 | Occasional posting (1-2x/week or less) with some relevant content |
| 3-4 | Rare posting, mostly reshares, or content unrelated to ICP |
| 1-2 | No activity, or activity that actively hurts positioning |

## Overall Profile Score Calculation

Calculate the overall score using weighted averages:

| Section | Weight | Multiplier |
|---------|--------|------------|
| Headline | CRITICAL | x3 |
| About Section | CRITICAL | x3 |
| Profile Photo | HIGH | x2 |
| Banner Image | MEDIUM | x1.5 |
| Featured Section | HIGH | x2 |
| Experience Section | MEDIUM | x1.5 |
| Skills & Endorsements | LOW-MEDIUM | x1 |
| Recommendations | HIGH | x2 |
| Activity & Content | HIGH | x2 |

**Formula:**
Total Weighted Score = Sum of (Section Score x Multiplier) / Sum of Multipliers

**Score interpretation:**
| Overall Score | Rating | Assessment |
|---------------|--------|------------|
| 9.0 - 10.0 | ELITE | Profile is a conversion machine. Minor tweaks only. |
| 7.5 - 8.9 | STRONG | Profile works well but has specific gaps reducing conversion. |
| 6.0 - 7.4 | AVERAGE | Profile is functional but not optimized — leaving significant opportunities on the table. |
| 4.0 - 5.9 | WEAK | Profile is not working as a sales tool. Major sections need rewriting. |
| 1.0 - 3.9 | CRITICAL | Profile is actively damaging your sales efforts. Full rebuild needed. |

## Priority Ranking Framework

After scoring, rank fixes by impact. Use this priority matrix:

**Priority 1 — Fix Immediately (biggest conversion impact):**
1. Headline rewrite (appears everywhere, affects search, first thing prospects read)
2. About section first 2 lines (the hook determines if anyone reads the rest)
3. Profile photo upgrade (no photo or bad photo = instant disqualification)

**Priority 2 — Fix This Week (strong conversion impact):**
4. About section full rewrite (the body of your sales page)
5. Featured section setup (your evidence shelf and CTA placement)
6. Banner image (visual reinforcement of positioning)

**Priority 3 — Fix This Month (credibility and discovery impact):**
7. Recommendations acquisition (social proof takes time but compounds)
8. Experience section rewrite (supporting credibility)
9. Skills & endorsements curation (search discoverability)

**Priority 4 — Ongoing (sustained impact):**
10. Content activity (requires consistency but pays off in inbound leads)
