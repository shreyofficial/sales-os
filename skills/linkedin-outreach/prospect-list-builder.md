# Prospect List Builder

## Purpose

Transform your Ideal Customer Profile into a live, actionable list of real humans you can reach out to on LinkedIn. This skill walks you through every method — free and paid — for finding, qualifying, organizing, and managing prospect lists so you always have a full pipeline of the right people to contact.

---

## Prerequisites

Before you begin, confirm you have completed:

- **ICP Architect** — You need a clearly defined Ideal Customer Profile (job titles, industries, company sizes, geographies, pain points) before you search for anyone.

Have your ICP document open and ready to reference throughout this process.

## Part 1: LinkedIn Sales Navigator Search Strategies

Sales Navigator is the most powerful prospecting tool on LinkedIn. If you have access, use these strategies to build laser-targeted lists.

### 1.1 Core Filters to Set First

Every search should start with these foundational filters:

| Filter | How to Use It |
|---|---|
| **Job Title** | Enter your ICP's exact title(s). Use the "Current" modifier — past titles add noise. |
| **Company Headcount** | Match your ICP's company size range (e.g., 11-50, 51-200, 201-500). |
| **Geography** | Set to the regions you serve. Start narrow, expand later. |
| **Industry** | Select 1-3 industries per search. Broader industry categories often work better than niche ones. |
| **Seniority Level** | Use this as a backup filter if job titles alone are too broad (e.g., VP, Director, C-Suite). |

### 1.2 Advanced Filters for Precision

Layer these on top of the core filters to narrow further:

- **Company Revenue** — If you only work with companies above a certain revenue threshold
- **Years in Current Position** — People who are 0-1 years in role are often actively making changes; people 3+ years are more established decision-makers
- **Posted on LinkedIn in Past 30 Days** — Filters for active users who will actually see your messages
- **Changed Jobs in Past 90 Days** — New hires often bring in new vendors and tools
- **Connections Of** — Enter a current client or strategic connection to find similar people in their network
- **Groups** — Filter for members of industry-specific LinkedIn groups

### 1.3 Boolean Search Strings for Sales Navigator

Boolean search lets you combine keywords with operators (AND, OR, NOT, quotation marks, parentheses) to create precise searches.

**Syntax Rules:**
- `"exact phrase"` — Matches the exact phrase
- `AND` — Both terms must be present
- `OR` — Either term can be present
- `NOT` — Excludes a term
- `()` — Groups terms together

**Template:**
```
("Title A" OR "Title B" OR "Title C") AND ("Industry Keyword A" OR "Industry Keyword B") NOT ("Unrelated Title" OR "Unrelated Keyword")
```

### 1.4 Boolean Search Strings by Vertical

**B2B SaaS / Technology:**
```
("VP of Marketing" OR "Head of Marketing" OR "Director of Marketing" OR "CMO" OR "Chief Marketing Officer") AND ("SaaS" OR "software" OR "tech" OR "B2B") NOT ("intern" OR "assistant" OR "coordinator" OR "freelance")
```

**E-Commerce / DTC Brands:**
```
("Founder" OR "CEO" OR "Head of E-Commerce" OR "Director of Growth" OR "DTC" OR "E-Commerce Manager") AND ("e-commerce" OR "ecommerce" OR "Shopify" OR "DTC" OR "direct to consumer" OR "online retail") NOT ("marketplace" OR "Amazon seller" OR "intern")
```

**Professional Services (Agencies, Consultants, Law Firms, Accounting):**
```
("Managing Partner" OR "Founder" OR "Principal" OR "Owner" OR "CEO" OR "Managing Director") AND ("agency" OR "consulting" OR "law firm" OR "accounting" OR "advisory" OR "professional services") NOT ("freelance" OR "intern" OR "virtual assistant")
```

**Healthcare / Medical:**
```
("Practice Owner" OR "Medical Director" OR "Office Manager" OR "CEO" OR "Administrator") AND ("healthcare" OR "medical practice" OR "dental" OR "clinic" OR "hospital" OR "health system") NOT ("student" OR "intern" OR "resident" OR "nursing assistant")
```

**Real Estate / Construction:**
```
("Broker" OR "Team Lead" OR "Owner" OR "Managing Director" OR "VP of Operations" OR "CEO") AND ("real estate" OR "commercial real estate" OR "property management" OR "construction" OR "general contractor" OR "development") NOT ("agent" OR "assistant" OR "intern" OR "leasing")
```

> **Tip:** Save each Boolean string as a saved search in Sales Navigator. Revisit weekly — new people matching your criteria join LinkedIn constantly.

## Part 2: Free LinkedIn Search Optimization

If you do not have Sales Navigator, you can still build strong prospect lists using LinkedIn's free search. The filters are more limited but the approach still works.

### 2.1 Free Search Filters Available

- **People** search (not "All" — always click "People" first)
- **Connections** — 1st, 2nd, 3rd+. Focus on 2nd-degree connections for warmer outreach.
- **Locations** — Filter by city, state, or country.
- **Current Company** — Search within specific companies you want to target.
- **Industry** — Broad industry categories.
- **School** — Useful for alumni-based outreach.
- **Keywords** — Entered in the main search bar; LinkedIn searches across headline, title, summary, and experience.

### 2.2 Free Search Keyword Strategies

Since free LinkedIn search does not support full Boolean, use these workarounds:

**Strategy 1: Headline Search**
Put your target title in quotes in the search bar: `"VP of Marketing"`. LinkedIn weights headline matches heavily, so the top results will be people with that exact title in their headline.

**Strategy 2: Title + Company Stacking**
Search for `"Director of Sales"` and then use the "Current Company" filter to search within a specific company or set of companies.

**Strategy 3: Industry Keyword Combos**
Search for `marketing agency founder` or `SaaS CEO` — LinkedIn will return profiles matching those keywords in various fields.

**Strategy 4: Google X-Ray Search**
Use Google to search LinkedIn with more precision than LinkedIn's own search allows:

```
site:linkedin.com/in/ "VP of Marketing" "SaaS" "San Francisco"
```

```
site:linkedin.com/in/ "Founder" "e-commerce" ("New York" OR "Los Angeles")
```

```
site:linkedin.com/in/ "Director of Operations" "healthcare" -recruiter -hiring
```

This is one of the most powerful free prospecting methods available. Google indexes LinkedIn profiles and supports full Boolean search.

### 2.3 Maximizing Free Search Results

- LinkedIn's free search returns approximately 1,000 results per query. Use specific keyword combinations to stay under this cap and get more relevant results.
- Run multiple narrow searches rather than one broad search.
- Log out of LinkedIn and use Google X-Ray search for unlimited results (you will not see connection degree, but you will get profile URLs).

## Part 3: Alternative Prospect Sources on LinkedIn

The search bar is not the only way to find prospects. These secondary sources are often higher quality because they come with built-in context.

### 3.1 "People Also Viewed" Sidebar

When you visit a prospect's profile, LinkedIn shows a "People Also Viewed" section on the right sidebar (desktop). These are people with similar titles, industries, and networks — essentially LinkedIn doing prospecting for you.

**How to use it:**
1. Find one ideal prospect who perfectly matches your ICP.
2. Visit their profile.
3. Review the 8-10 "People Also Viewed" profiles.
4. Visit the best matches — each of their profiles will show another "People Also Viewed" list.
5. Repeat. This "prospect chaining" method can generate 50-100 qualified prospects from a single starting point.

### 3.2 Company Page Followers and Employees

If you have identified target companies:
1. Go to the company's LinkedIn page.
2. Click "People" to see all employees.
3. Use the filter bar to narrow by title, location, or keyword.
4. Identify the decision-makers within each company.

For companies with large headcounts, filter by department (Marketing, Sales, Operations, etc.) to find the right stakeholders.

### 3.3 LinkedIn Group Members

Industry-specific LinkedIn groups are goldmines for prospects:
1. Join 5-10 groups relevant to your ICP's industry or role.
2. Go to the group and click "Members."
3. Browse or search within the member list.
4. Group members share a common interest — use this as a conversation starter in your outreach.

> **Note:** You can message fellow group members even if you are not connected (on some LinkedIn plans). Group membership is also a great personalization hook.

### 3.4 LinkedIn Event Attendees

People who attend LinkedIn events (webinars, live sessions, networking events) in your industry are actively engaged professionals.

1. Search for events relevant to your ICP's industry.
2. Click "Attend" on relevant upcoming or past events.
3. View the attendee list.
4. Attendees of industry events are warmer prospects — they are actively investing time in professional development in the area you serve.

### 3.5 Post Engagement Mining

Find influencers or companies in your ICP's space and look at who engages with their posts:
1. Identify 5-10 LinkedIn influencers your ICP likely follows.
2. Review their recent posts.
3. Click on the likes and comments.
4. The people engaging are active LinkedIn users interested in that topic — prime prospects.

### 3.6 LinkedIn Alumni Search

If you share a university with your ICP:
1. Go to your school's LinkedIn page.
2. Click "Alumni."
3. Filter by location, company, job title, and skills.
4. Alumni connections have a natural rapport — mention the shared school in your outreach.

## Part 4: Lead List Hygiene — Qualifying Before You Add

Not everyone who matches a search filter belongs on your list. Before adding a prospect, run them through this quick qualification check.

### 4.1 The 60-Second Qualification Check

For each prospect, review the following on their profile:

| Criterion | What to Check | Pass / Fail |
|---|---|---|
| **Title Match** | Does their current title match your ICP? (Not a past title) | Must pass |
| **Company Size** | Does their company fit your ICP's company size range? (Check "About" section or company page) | Must pass |
| **Industry Fit** | Is the company in an industry you serve? | Must pass |
| **Geography** | Are they in a location you can serve? | Must pass |
| **Activity Recency** | Have they posted, commented, or been active in the last 90 days? | Preferred but not required |
| **Profile Completeness** | Do they have a profile photo, headline, and summary? Incomplete profiles often mean inactive users. | Preferred |
| **Decision-Making Authority** | Based on their title and company size, are they likely a decision-maker or influencer for what you sell? | Must pass |

**Rule of thumb:** A prospect must pass all "Must pass" criteria and ideally 1-2 of the "Preferred" criteria.

### 4.2 Red Flags to Skip

- Profile has not been updated in 2+ years
- Headline says "Open to Work" or "Seeking Opportunities" (unless you sell recruiting/career services)
- Company shows 0-1 employees on LinkedIn (could be a solo freelancer, not a business)
- Job title includes "intern," "assistant," "coordinator," or "junior" (unless that is your ICP)
- Profile is clearly a personal brand / influencer account with no actual operating business

## Part 5: List Sizing and Batching Strategy

### 5.1 Ideal List Sizes

Do not build a list of 500 people and blast them all at once. This leads to generic messaging, LinkedIn restrictions, and poor results.

**Recommended approach:**

| Stage | List Size | Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| **Active Outreach Batch** | 20-30 prospects | New batch every 1-2 weeks |
| **Warm-Up List** (engagement before DM) | 50-100 prospects | Ongoing, rotate monthly |
| **Master Prospect Pipeline** | 200-500 prospects | Refreshed monthly |

### 5.2 Batching Strategy

1. **Build your Master Pipeline** of 200-500 qualified prospects.
2. **Segment into batches** of 20-30 based on shared characteristics (same industry, same title, same company size).
3. **Craft batch-specific messaging** — tailor your connection requests and DM openers to each batch's shared characteristics.
4. **Work one batch at a time.** Send connection requests on Monday-Tuesday. Follow up with accepted connections Wednesday-Friday. Start the next batch the following week.
5. **Track results per batch.** Which batches have the highest acceptance and reply rates? Double down on those segments.

## Part 6: The Warm Prospect Identification Method

Warm prospects are people who already have some awareness of or connection to you. They convert at 3-5x the rate of purely cold prospects. Identify them using these signals:

### 6.1 Profile Viewers

- Check "Who Viewed Your Profile" weekly (free LinkedIn shows the last 5; Premium/Sales Navigator shows all).
- Anyone who views your profile and matches your ICP should be added to your priority outreach list.
- When you reach out, you can reference the profile view: "I noticed you checked out my profile — [relevant question or comment]."

### 6.2 Content Engagers

- Review who likes and comments on your posts.
- Anyone who engages with your content and matches your ICP is a warm prospect.
- Reference their engagement in your outreach.

### 6.3 2nd-Degree Connections Through Strategic People

- Look at who your best clients and partners are connected to.
- 2nd-degree connections through a relevant mutual connection are significantly warmer than 3rd-degree or out-of-network.
- You can reference the mutual connection in your outreach (with permission, or simply say "I see we're both connected to [Name]").

### 6.4 Inbound Connection Requests

- People who send YOU connection requests and match your ICP are the warmest prospects of all.
- Always accept and respond within 24 hours with a personalized message.

## Part 7: Tools for List Building (Generic Recommendations)

You do not need paid tools to build prospect lists, but they can significantly speed up the process.

| Tool Category | What It Does | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **LinkedIn Sales Navigator** | Advanced search, lead lists, saved searches, InMail credits | LinkedIn's premium offering |
| **B2B Contact Databases** | Search for prospects by title, company, industry, and get email addresses | Apollo, ZoomInfo, Lusha, Seamless.ai |
| **LinkedIn Automation / Scraping** | Extract search results into spreadsheets, automate profile visits | PhantomBuster, Dux-Soup, Octopus CRM |
| **Enrichment Tools** | Add missing data (email, phone, company info) to existing prospect lists | Clearbit, FullContact, Hunter.io |
| **CRM Integration** | Sync LinkedIn prospects directly to your CRM | HubSpot LinkedIn integration, Salesforce |

> **Important:** LinkedIn automation tools violate LinkedIn's Terms of Service. Use them at your own risk and always keep volume conservative. Manual, targeted outreach consistently outperforms automated mass outreach.

## Part 8: List Organization — Segmentation and Tagging

### 8.1 Recommended Prospect List Fields

Track the following for every prospect:

| Field | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Full Name | Identification |
| LinkedIn Profile URL | Quick access for outreach |
| Job Title | Confirm ICP match |
| Company Name | Context for personalization |
| Company Size | Segment by company size |
| Industry | Segment by vertical |
| Location | Timezone and geo-targeting |
| Connection Degree | 1st, 2nd, 3rd — affects outreach strategy |
| Mutual Connections | Personalization hook |
| Recent Activity / Post Topic | Personalization hook |
| Warm Signal | Profile view, content engagement, etc. |
| Batch Number | Which outreach batch they belong to |
| Status | Not Contacted, Connection Sent, Connected, DM Sent, Replied, Call Booked, Not Interested |
| Date Added | Track list freshness |
| Notes | Any relevant context for personalization |

### 8.2 Segmentation Tags

Tag each prospect with one or more of the following to enable targeted messaging:

- **By Industry:** [Industry A], [Industry B], [Industry C]
- **By Title/Seniority:** C-Suite, VP, Director, Manager, Founder
- **By Company Size:** Small (1-50), Mid (51-200), Large (201-1000), Enterprise (1000+)
- **By Warmth:** Cold, Warm (profile view), Warm (content engagement), Warm (mutual connection), Hot (inbound)
- **By Pain Point:** If your ICP has multiple pain points, tag by the pain point most relevant to each prospect

### 8.3 Simple Tracking Without a CRM

If you do not have a CRM, use a spreadsheet (Google Sheets, Airtable, or Notion database) with the fields above. Create separate tabs or views for:

1. **Master List** — All qualified prospects
2. **Active Batch** — The 20-30 you are currently reaching out to
3. **Warm Prospects** — Priority outreach list
4. **Replied / In Conversation** — Active conversations
5. **Booked Calls** — Prospects who have scheduled a call
6. **Not Now** — Revisit in 30-90 days
7. **Not Interested** — Archive (do not delete; revisit in 6-12 months)

## Part 9: Output Format

After completing this skill, you will have a **Prospect Targeting Playbook** that includes:

1. **Search Strings** — 3-5 Boolean search strings tailored to your ICP, ready to paste into Sales Navigator or Google X-Ray
2. **Filter Combinations** — Documented filter settings for each search
3. **Source Channels** — List of the specific sources you will pull prospects from (Sales Navigator, Google X-Ray, groups, events, company pages, People Also Viewed, content engagement)
4. **Qualification Criteria** — Your pass/fail checklist customized to your ICP
5. **List Management Strategy** — Spreadsheet or CRM template with fields, segments, tags, and batch plan
6. **First Active Batch** — An initial list of 20-30 qualified, segmented, and tagged prospects ready for outreach
7. **Warm Prospect List** — 10-20 warm prospects identified through profile views, content engagement, mutual connections, or inbound requests
