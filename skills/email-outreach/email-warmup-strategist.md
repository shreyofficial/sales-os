# Email Warm-Up Strategist

## Purpose

Build a comprehensive warm-up plan for new sending domains and inboxes. Sending cold email from a new domain or inbox without proper warm-up is one of the fastest ways to get blacklisted and land in spam permanently. This skill produces a week-by-week warm-up schedule, inbox and domain strategy, monitoring protocols, and troubleshooting guides.

---

## Why Warm-Up Is Critical

### How Email Providers Judge You

```
Email providers (Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo, etc.) use reputation systems
to decide whether your email lands in the inbox, promotions, or spam.

REPUTATION IS BUILT ON:
1. Sending history — How many emails have you sent from this domain/inbox?
   New domains have ZERO history = no reputation = suspicious.

2. Engagement signals — Do recipients open, reply to, and interact with
   your emails? Or do they ignore, delete, and mark as spam?

3. Volume patterns — Is your sending consistent or erratic? Sudden
   spikes from a new domain screams "spammer."

4. Bounce rate — Are you sending to valid addresses? High bounces
   suggest a purchased or scraped list.

5. Spam complaints — Are recipients hitting "Report spam"? Even
   0.1% complaint rate is the danger zone.

THE CORE PROBLEM WITH NEW DOMAINS/INBOXES:
- Zero sending history = zero reputation
- Email providers default to suspicion for unknown senders
- Sending 200 cold emails on day 1 from a new inbox will likely
  result in 80%+ of them going to spam
- Once you're flagged as spam, it's 10x harder to recover than
  to build reputation correctly from the start

THE ANALOGY:
A new domain/inbox is like a new driver with no driving record.
Insurance companies (email providers) assume the worst until you
prove yourself through consistent, responsible behavior over time.
```

## The Warm-Up Timeline

### Week-by-Week Plan (6 Weeks)

```
============================================
   WEEK 1: FOUNDATION (Days 1-7)
============================================
Daily volume: 5-10 emails per day
Recipients: ONLY known contacts — colleagues, friends, existing
            clients, business partners, newsletter subscriptions
Cold email: 0%
Activities:
- Send personal emails to 5-10 contacts daily
- Reply to emails you receive
- Subscribe to 3-5 industry newsletters (generates inbound email)
- Send and receive emails within your own organization
- If using Google Workspace: use the inbox for Google Drive sharing,
  Calendar invites, and Google Meet links
Goal: Establish the inbox as a real, active email account
Monitoring: Check that all sent emails land in recipients' inboxes
            (ask friends/colleagues to confirm)

============================================
   WEEK 2: BUILDING (Days 8-14)
============================================
Daily volume: 15-25 emails per day
Recipients: 60% warm contacts, 40% cold prospects
Cold email: Start with highest-quality, most-verified email addresses
Activities:
- Continue personal email activity (5-10/day)
- Add 10-15 cold emails per day to highly verified addresses
- Reply to any cold email responses immediately
- Continue newsletter subscriptions (open and click links in them)
- Send at least 2-3 emails that generate replies (ask questions
  to friends/colleagues to encourage back-and-forth)
Goal: Introduce cold sending at low volume while maintaining
      strong engagement signals
Monitoring: Track open rates on cold emails — should be 40%+
            Watch for any deliverability warnings

============================================
   WEEK 3: EXPANDING (Days 15-21)
============================================
Daily volume: 30-50 emails per day
Recipients: 40% warm contacts, 60% cold prospects
Cold email: Increasing volume, still using best-verified addresses
Activities:
- 15-20 cold emails per day
- 10-15 warm/personal emails per day
- Continue newsletter engagement
- Start A/B testing subject lines (small scale)
- Track bounce rates — if above 3%, pause cold sends and verify list
Goal: Build cold sending volume while maintaining engagement metrics
Monitoring: Open rate should remain above 35%
            Bounce rate must stay below 3%
            Check Google Postmaster Tools for reputation changes

============================================
   WEEK 4: SCALING (Days 22-28)
============================================
Daily volume: 50-75 emails per day
Recipients: 25% warm contacts, 75% cold prospects
Cold email: 40-55 per day
Activities:
- Full cold email campaigns at moderate volume
- Maintain some warm sending for engagement balance
- Monitor all metrics daily
- If metrics are healthy, increase volume by 5-10 per day
- If metrics are declining, hold volume or decrease
Goal: Approach operational sending volume
Monitoring: All metrics (open rate, bounce rate, spam complaints)
            Google Postmaster reputation (should be Medium or High)

============================================
   WEEK 5: APPROACHING TARGET (Days 29-35)
============================================
Daily volume: 75-100 emails per day
Recipients: 15% warm contacts, 85% cold prospects
Cold email: 65-85 per day
Activities:
- Full cold email campaigns
- Continue monitoring closely
- Begin second inbox warm-up if planning multi-inbox strategy
Goal: Reach near-target volume with stable metrics
Monitoring: Same as Week 4 — any metric degradation = reduce volume

============================================
   WEEK 6+: TARGET VOLUME (Days 36+)
============================================
Daily volume: 100-150 emails per day (maximum per inbox)
Recipients: 10% warm contacts, 90% cold prospects
Cold email: At full operational volume
Activities:
- Full campaigns running
- Ongoing warm email mixed in for engagement health
- Continuous monitoring (becomes routine)
Goal: Sustainable, high-deliverability cold email at target volume
Monitoring: Weekly metric reviews, monthly Google Postmaster checks

IMPORTANT: These numbers are per inbox. If you need to send 300 emails/day,
you need 3 inboxes, each following this warm-up independently.
```

## Manual Warm-Up Activities

### Daily Actions During Warm-Up Phase

```
WEEK 1-2 DAILY ROUTINE (10-15 minutes per inbox):

Morning:
□ Open the inbox and check for any new emails
□ Reply to 2-3 emails (real replies, not automated)
□ Send 2-3 personal emails to colleagues/friends
□ Open and read 1-2 newsletter emails (click a link in at least 1)

Midday:
□ Send 2-3 more personal emails
□ Reply to any incoming emails
□ Star/archive emails to show active inbox management
□ If using Gmail: move 1-2 emails between tabs (shows inbox engagement)

Afternoon:
□ Send remaining daily emails
□ Check for replies and respond
□ Review sent folder for any bounce-backs

WEEK 3+ DAILY ROUTINE (5 minutes per inbox):
□ Check for replies to cold emails — respond immediately
□ Send 5-10 warm emails
□ Reply to any incoming messages
□ Monitor metrics in your cold email tool
```

### Engagement-Boosting Tactics

```
1. SEND EMAILS THAT GUARANTEE REPLIES:
   - Email colleagues with questions they'll answer
   - Send calendar invites or meeting requests
   - Forward relevant articles to contacts with a question
   - Ask clients for feedback or testimonials

2. CREATE BACK-AND-FORTH CONVERSATIONS:
   - Multi-email threads show email providers this is a real account
   - Aim for 3-5 back-and-forth exchanges per day during warm-up
   - Even short replies count ("Thanks!" "Got it!" "Makes sense.")

3. ENGAGE WITH INBOUND EMAIL:
   - Open newsletters and click links (shows active reading behavior)
   - Reply to promotional emails with "unsubscribe" (ironic, but it's engagement)
   - Move emails to folders or labels (shows active inbox management)

4. USE THE INBOX FOR LEGITIMATE BUSINESS:
   - Sign up for SaaS tool trials using this inbox
   - Use it for Google Drive/Dropbox sharing
   - Register for webinars with this email
   - Use it for any business correspondence during the warm-up period
```

## Automated Warm-Up Tools

### How They Work

```
Automated warm-up tools simulate natural email behavior by:
1. Sending emails from your inbox to a network of other inboxes
2. Those inboxes automatically open, reply to, and engage with your emails
3. If your email lands in spam, the tool moves it to the inbox
   (training the email provider to trust you)
4. This creates a pattern of positive engagement signals

THE RESULT: Faster reputation building than manual warm-up alone.
```

### Tool Comparison

```
LEMWARM (by Lemlist):
- Integrated with Lemlist's cold email platform
- Network of 20,000+ real inboxes
- Automatic spam rescue (moves from spam to inbox)
- Cost: Included with Lemlist subscription
- Best for: Lemlist users

WARMBOX:
- Standalone warm-up tool (works with any inbox)
- Multiple warm-up "recipes" (speed, recovery, warm maintenance)
- Detailed reputation reporting
- Cost: $15-50/month per inbox
- Best for: Users needing dedicated warm-up

MAILREACH:
- Standalone warm-up + deliverability monitoring
- Real-time reputation score
- Works with Gmail, Outlook, and custom SMTP
- Cost: $25-50/month per inbox
- Best for: Users who want monitoring alongside warm-up

INSTANTLY (built-in warm-up):
- Integrated warm-up in Instantly's cold email platform
- Large warm-up network
- Automatic campaign pausing if reputation drops
- Cost: Included with Instantly subscription
- Best for: Instantly users

SMARTLEAD (built-in warm-up):
- Integrated warm-up in Smartlead
- Warm-up runs continuously even during campaigns
- Cost: Included with Smartlead subscription
- Best for: Smartlead users
```

### Automated Warm-Up Best Practices

```
DO:
- Start automated warm-up the day you create the inbox
- Run warm-up for at least 2 weeks BEFORE sending any cold email
- Keep warm-up running even after you start cold campaigns
  (it maintains positive engagement signals)
- Monitor the warm-up tool's deliverability score/reputation metrics
- Set warm-up volume to 30-40 emails/day during warm-up phase
- Reduce warm-up volume to 10-20/day once cold sending starts
  (total daily volume = warm-up + cold should stay within limits)

DON'T:
- Don't rely ONLY on automated warm-up — do manual activities too
- Don't set warm-up volume too high (100+/day from a new inbox is suspicious)
- Don't stop warm-up the day you start cold sending
- Don't ignore the tool's warnings or reputation scores
- Don't use warm-up as a substitute for good email hygiene
  (list verification, proper authentication, quality content)
```

## Multiple Inbox Strategy

### Why Multiple Inboxes

```
PROBLEM: Each inbox can safely send 30-50 cold emails per day.
If you need to send 150-200 per day, one inbox isn't enough.

SOLUTION: Use 3-5 inboxes that rotate sending.

BENEFITS:
1. Spread volume across inboxes = lower risk per inbox
2. If one inbox gets flagged, others continue working
3. A/B test from different sender names/personas
4. Different inboxes for different ICP segments
5. Built-in redundancy — never fully shut down if one inbox has issues

HOW IT WORKS:
- 3 inboxes × 50 emails/day each = 150 cold emails/day capacity
- 5 inboxes × 40 emails/day each = 200 cold emails/day capacity
- Each inbox is on the same domain (or spread across 2-3 domains)
- Cold email tools like Instantly and Smartlead automatically rotate
  between inboxes within a campaign
```

### Inbox Setup Guide

```
FOR 3-INBOX SETUP:

Inbox 1: firstname@secondarydomain.com (primary sender)
Inbox 2: firstname.lastname@secondarydomain.com (variant)
Inbox 3: firstname@secondarydomain2.com (different domain for safety)

FOR 5-INBOX SETUP:

Inbox 1: firstname@secondarydomain.com
Inbox 2: firstname.lastname@secondarydomain.com
Inbox 3: firstname@secondarydomain2.com
Inbox 4: f.lastname@secondarydomain.com
Inbox 5: firstname@secondarydomain3.com

EACH INBOX NEEDS:
□ Separate Google Workspace or Microsoft 365 account
□ Its own profile photo (use a real photo, not stock)
□ A professional signature
□ Individual warm-up (each inbox goes through the 6-week warm-up)
□ Its own sending limits tracked independently
□ SPF, DKIM, DMARC configured for each domain
```

## Domain Strategy

### Never Risk Your Primary Domain

```
CRITICAL RULE: NEVER send cold email from your primary business domain.

YOUR PRIMARY DOMAIN (e.g., yourbrand.com):
- Used for: Website, employee email, client communication, marketing
- Risk of cold email: If your cold outreach triggers spam flags, your
  entire company email could be affected. Clients might stop receiving
  your invoices. Partners might not see your contracts. Applicants might
  not get your offer letters.
- VERDICT: NEVER use for cold outreach.

SECONDARY DOMAIN(S) (e.g., yourbrandteam.com):
- Used for: Cold outreach only
- Risk: If flagged, only cold email is affected. Business email continues.
- If the domain gets burned, you can switch to a new secondary domain
  without any impact on your primary business.
- VERDICT: ALWAYS use for cold outreach.
```

### Secondary Domain Naming Conventions

```
GUIDELINES:
- Should be clearly related to your brand (so replies can be traced back)
- Should look professional (not spammy or suspicious)
- Should be a .com domain (other TLDs have lower trust)
- Register through the same registrar as your primary domain
- Buy 2-3 secondary domains so you have backups

NAMING PATTERNS:

Pattern 1: [brand]team.com
Example: acmeteam.com
Best for: Professional services, consultancies

Pattern 2: get[brand].com
Example: getacme.com
Best for: Product companies, SaaS

Pattern 3: [brand]hq.com
Example: acmehq.com
Best for: Companies with a strong brand name

Pattern 4: try[brand].com
Example: tryacme.com
Best for: Offering trials, demos, or free assessments

Pattern 5: [brand]growth.com or [brand]labs.com
Example: acmegrowth.com
Best for: Growth-stage companies, agencies

Pattern 6: meet[brand].com or hello[brand].com
Example: meetacme.com
Best for: Friendly, approachable outreach

WHAT TO AVOID:
- Random strings: xjk7brand.com (looks like spam)
- Numbers: brand123.com (looks like spam)
- Hyphens: brand-team.com (harder to type, less professional)
- Non-.com TLDs: brand.io, brand.xyz (lower trust for cold email)
- Anything misleading: brand-official.com (looks phishing-esque)
```

### Domain Setup Checklist

```
FOR EACH SECONDARY DOMAIN:

□ 1. Register the domain (GoDaddy, Namecheap, Cloudflare, Google Domains)
□ 2. Wait at least 2 weeks before sending any email
     (domain age check — brand new domains are suspicious)
□ 3. Set up Google Workspace or Microsoft 365 for email hosting
□ 4. Create 1-3 email accounts on the domain
□ 5. Configure SPF record in DNS
□ 6. Configure DKIM in DNS
□ 7. Configure DMARC in DNS (start with p=none)
□ 8. Set up a basic landing page on the domain
     (a blank domain with no website looks suspicious)
     Even a simple one-page site with your company info is enough
□ 9. Add the domain to Google Postmaster Tools for monitoring
□ 10. Begin warm-up protocol (6-week plan above)
□ 11. Set up custom tracking domain for your cold email tool
□ 12. Connect to your cold email platform
```

## Monitoring Warm-Up Progress

### Metrics to Track Weekly

```
│ Metric                    │ Healthy    │ Warning    │ Critical    │
│───────────────────────────│────────────│────────────│─────────────│
│ Open rate (warm-up)       │ 40%+       │ 25-40%     │ <25%        │
│ Open rate (cold)          │ 35%+       │ 20-35%     │ <20%        │
│ Reply rate (warm-up)      │ 30%+       │ 15-30%     │ <15%        │
│ Bounce rate               │ <1%        │ 1-3%       │ >3%         │
│ Spam complaint rate       │ <0.05%     │ 0.05-0.1%  │ >0.1%       │
│ Google Postmaster rep.    │ High       │ Medium     │ Low/Bad     │
│ Warm-up tool score        │ 90+        │ 70-90      │ <70         │
│ Inbox placement (seed)    │ Primary    │ Promotions │ Spam        │
```

### When to Speed Up vs. Slow Down

```
SPEED UP (increase volume by 10-15 emails/day) when:
- Open rate consistently above 40% for 7+ days
- Zero bounces for 5+ days
- Google Postmaster reputation is High
- Warm-up tool shows 90+ score
- Reply rate on cold emails is above average
- No spam complaints in the last 100+ sends

SLOW DOWN (reduce volume by 25-50%) when:
- Open rate drops below 30%
- Bounce rate exceeds 2%
- Google Postmaster reputation drops to Medium
- Warm-up tool score drops below 80
- You notice emails landing in Promotions instead of Primary

STOP COLD SENDING IMMEDIATELY when:
- Open rate drops below 15% (deliverability crisis)
- Bounce rate exceeds 5%
- Google Postmaster reputation drops to Low or Bad
- You find yourself on a blacklist
- Spam complaints exceed 0.1%
- Your warm-up tool shows a sudden reputation drop

RECOVERY STEPS (if you need to stop):
1. Pause all cold email campaigns
2. Continue warm-up activities only (manual + automated)
3. Run inbox placement tests (send to seed accounts)
4. Check blacklists and resolve any listings
5. Review your email content for spam triggers
6. Verify your email list again
7. Wait 1-2 weeks for reputation to stabilize
8. Resume cold sending at 50% of previous volume
9. Gradually ramp back up only when metrics recover
```

## What to Do If Warm-Up Goes Wrong

### Problem: Sudden Deliverability Drop

```
SYMPTOMS:
- Open rates plummet from 40% to 10%
- Warm-up tool shows declining score
- Seed test emails land in spam
- Google Postmaster reputation drops

DIAGNOSTIC STEPS:
1. Check blacklists (MXToolbox) — are you listed?
2. Check DNS records — did something change or expire?
3. Check your email content — did you use new copy with spam triggers?
4. Check your list — did a batch of bad emails cause bounces?
5. Check your sending platform — any service issues?

COMMON CAUSES:
- Sent too many cold emails too fast during warm-up
- List quality issue (high bounces triggered reputation damage)
- Content triggered spam filters (new template with bad words)
- IP/domain got listed on a blacklist (could be through shared IP)
- DNS records were accidentally modified or deleted

RECOVERY PLAN:
Day 1-2: Stop all cold sending. Continue manual warm-up only.
Day 3-7: Fix root cause (delist, fix DNS, clean list, fix content).
Day 7-14: Resume automated warm-up at low volume. Monitor closely.
Day 14-21: Gradually reintroduce cold sending at 25% previous volume.
Day 21-30: Scale back to normal if metrics stabilize.
```

### Problem: Blacklisted During Warm-Up

```
SYMPTOMS:
- MXToolbox shows one or more blacklist hits
- Bounce messages mention blacklist
- Deliverability drops sharply

COMMON BLACKLISTS AND IMPACT:
│ Blacklist      │ Impact Level │ Self-Removal?     │
│────────────────│──────────────│───────────────────│
│ Spamhaus SBL   │ Critical     │ Yes (with cause)  │
│ Spamhaus XBL   │ High         │ Yes (automated)   │
│ Barracuda      │ High         │ Yes (self-service) │
│ SpamCop        │ Medium       │ Auto (24-48 hrs)   │
│ SORBS          │ Medium       │ Yes (self-service) │
│ CBL            │ Medium       │ Yes (self-service) │

STEPS:
1. Identify which blacklist(s) you're on
2. Visit the blacklist's website for their removal process
3. Fix the root cause BEFORE requesting removal
4. Submit delisting request
5. Wait 24-72 hours for processing
6. Re-check after the waiting period
7. If still listed, investigate whether the root cause persists
8. After delisting: resume warm-up from Week 1 levels (reset the clock)
```

## The Maintenance Phase

### Long-Term Reputation Management

```
After completing the 6-week warm-up, your inbox isn't "done."
Reputation is ongoing — it can degrade if you're not careful.

ONGOING MAINTENANCE (forever):

DAILY:
□ Keep warm-up running (10-20 warm-up emails/day alongside cold sends)
□ Reply to all responses within SLA
□ Monitor bounce rate on active campaigns

WEEKLY:
□ Review key metrics (open rate, bounce rate, spam complaints)
□ Run inbox placement seed test (send to your test accounts)
□ Clean any bounced addresses from your lists

MONTHLY:
□ Check Google Postmaster reputation
□ Run blacklist check (MXToolbox)
□ Verify DNS records are still correct
□ Review and re-verify any email lists older than 90 days
□ Check warm-up tool health scores

QUARTERLY:
□ Full deliverability audit (use Email Deliverability Auditor skill)
□ Re-evaluate whether you need additional inboxes or domains
□ Review sending volume vs. capacity across all inboxes
□ Archive inactive inboxes (unused inboxes lose reputation)
```

### Volume Consistency Rules

```
RULE 1: Avoid volume spikes
Bad: 0 emails Monday, 300 emails Tuesday, 0 Wednesday
Good: 80 emails Monday, 85 Tuesday, 75 Wednesday

RULE 2: Keep weekday/weekend ratios natural
Bad: 100/day weekdays, 100/day weekends (looks automated)
Good: 80-100/day weekdays, 10-20/day weekends (looks human)

RULE 3: Don't go dark for extended periods
Bad: Send 100/day for 2 months, then 0/day for 3 weeks (vacation)
Good: Reduce to 20-30/day during slow periods, never to 0

RULE 4: Scale gradually, always
Bad: Jump from 50/day to 200/day because you have a big list
Good: Increase by 10-15/day each week until reaching target
```

- [ ] Domain strategy is defined (primary vs. secondary domains)
- [ ] Secondary domain naming follows best practices

- [ ] Number of inboxes is calculated based on target daily volume
- [ ] A complete week-by-week warm-up schedule is provided (6 weeks minimum)
- [ ] Daily warm-up activities are specified for each phase
- [ ] Both manual and automated warm-up approaches are covered
- [ ] Warm-up tool recommendation matches the user's email platform
- [ ] Monitoring metrics and thresholds are defined (healthy, warning, critical)
- [ ] Clear guidance on when to speed up, slow down, or stop sending
- [ ] Troubleshooting guides are provided for common warm-up problems
- [ ] Blacklist recovery process is documented
- [ ] Long-term maintenance plan extends beyond the warm-up period
- [ ] Volume consistency rules are specified
- [ ] The plan is realistic for the user's resources and timeline
