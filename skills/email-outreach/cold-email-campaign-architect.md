# Cold Email Campaign Architect

## Purpose

Design end-to-end cold email campaigns from strategy through execution. Each campaign is a self-contained outreach system targeting one ICP segment with one offer. This skill produces the complete campaign plan — targeting, messaging angle, sequence structure, personalization approach, volume plan, and success metrics — so the user can launch with confidence.

---

## Core Principle: One Campaign, One ICP, One Offer

Every campaign must follow this rule without exception:

```
ONE Campaign = ONE ICP Segment + ONE Core Offer + ONE Primary Angle

Why? Because:
- Different ICPs have different pain points — mixing them dilutes relevance
- Different offers appeal to different motivations — mixing them confuses the reader
- Different angles require different proof points — mixing them weakens credibility
- A/B testing is impossible when too many variables are in play
- Performance data becomes meaningless when audiences are mixed

If you want to target 3 ICP segments with 2 offers each,
that's 6 separate campaigns — not 1 campaign with 6 variations.
```

## Step 1: Define the Campaign Goal

### Primary Goal Options

Every campaign needs ONE primary goal. Choose from:

```
1. Book a meeting/discovery call
   → Best for: high-ticket services, consultative sales, complex B2B
   → Metric: meetings booked per 100 emails sent

2. Get a reply (start a conversation)
   → Best for: relationship-building, long sales cycles, enterprise
   → Metric: positive reply rate

3. Drive to a specific action (webinar signup, demo request, resource download)
   → Best for: product-led growth, lower-ticket offers, lead nurturing
   → Metric: click-through rate and conversion rate

4. Generate referrals (get introduced to the right person)
   → Best for: when you're unsure of the decision-maker, large organizations
   → Metric: referral rate

5. Re-engage cold leads
   → Best for: leads who went dark, past prospects, old pipeline
   → Metric: re-engagement rate (reply rate from previously cold leads)
```

### Questions to Define the Goal

```
1. What is the single most important outcome you want from this campaign?
2. What does success look like in numbers? (e.g., 10 meetings/month, 5% reply rate)
3. What's your average deal size? (This determines how much effort per lead is justified.)
4. What's your current sales cycle length? (This affects urgency in messaging.)
5. What's the next step after someone responds positively? (Call? Demo? Proposal?)
```

## Step 2: Select the ICP Segment

### Segment Selection Criteria

Pull from the ICP Architect output. For this campaign, select ONE segment based on:

```
PRIORITIZATION MATRIX:

                High Pain Level     Low Pain Level
High Access  |  START HERE (A)   |  Good backup (B)   |
Low Access   |  Worth it if      |  Skip for now (D)  |
             |  high value (C)   |                    |

A = High pain + easy to reach = highest reply rates, fastest results
B = Easy to reach but less urgent pain = decent volume, lower conversion
C = High pain but hard to reach = high conversion if you get through, low volume
D = Low pain + hard to reach = waste of time for cold email

Select the segment that falls in quadrant A first.
```

```

─────────────────────────────

Desired State: [What they want — the outcome your offer delivers]
Buying Trigger: [What event or situation makes them ready to buy NOW]
```

## Step 3: Choose the Campaign Angle

### The 8 Campaign Angles

Each angle approaches the prospect from a different direction. Choose the one that best fits your offer and the prospect's current situation:

```
1. THE PROBLEM ANGLE
   "You have [specific problem] and it's costing you [specific cost]"
   → Best when: the pain is acute and they know they have it
   → Example: "Most [ICP TITLE]s at [COMPANY SIZE] companies waste
     15+ hours/week on [TASK] — here's how [SIMILAR COMPANY] eliminated it"

2. THE RESULT ANGLE
   "We got [specific result] for [similar company]"
   → Best when: you have strong case studies with quantifiable outcomes
   → Example: "[SIMILAR COMPANY] increased [METRIC] by [X]% in [TIMEFRAME]
     using our [SERVICE]. Thought this might be relevant for [COMPANY]."

3. THE INSIGHT ANGLE
   "I noticed something about [their company] that could be improved"
   → Best when: you can do research and find genuine opportunities
   → Example: "I was looking at [COMPANY]'s [specific area] and noticed
     [specific observation]. We've helped [X] similar companies fix this."

4. THE TRIGGER EVENT ANGLE
   "Congrats on [event] — here's how to capitalize on it"
   → Best when: you can tie your outreach to a real event (funding, hire, launch)
   → Example: "Saw [COMPANY] just raised Series B — congrats. When [SIMILAR COMPANY]
     was at this stage, they used [SERVICE] to [RESULT]. Worth exploring?"

5. THE MUTUAL CONNECTION ANGLE
   "[Mutual contact] suggested I reach out"
   → Best when: you genuinely have a warm introduction path
   → Example: "[MUTUAL CONTACT] mentioned you're looking to [GOAL].
     We helped them [RESULT] and they thought we might be able to help you too."

6. THE PEER COMPARISON ANGLE
   "Your competitors are doing [thing] — are you?"
   → Best when: there's a competitive dynamic and FOMO is a motivator
   → Example: "3 of the top 10 [INDUSTRY] companies now use [APPROACH/SERVICE]
     to [BENEFIT]. Curious if [COMPANY] has explored this."

7. THE RESOURCE ANGLE
   "I created [resource] that's relevant to [their challenge]"
   → Best when: you have a lead magnet, report, or tool that provides standalone value
   → Example: "We just published a benchmark report on [TOPIC] with data from
     [X] [INDUSTRY] companies. Thought it'd be useful for your [INITIATIVE]."

8. THE DIRECT ANGLE
   "We do [thing]. Here's a result. Interested?"
   → Best when: your offer is straightforward and the value is self-evident
   → Example: "We help [ICP TITLE]s at [COMPANY SIZE] companies [SPECIFIC OUTCOME].
     Last client saw [RESULT] in [TIMEFRAME]. Worth 15 minutes to explore?"
```

## Step 4: Design the Sequence Architecture

### Standard Sequence Structure

```
RECOMMENDED: 5-7 emails over 21-28 days

Email 1 — Day 1:   The Opener (lead angle, primary value proposition)
Email 2 — Day 3:   The Value Add (different proof point or resource)
Email 3 — Day 7:   The Social Proof (case study or specific result)
Email 4 — Day 11:  The Different Angle (approach from new direction)
Email 5 — Day 16:  The Question (thought-provoking, re-engage attention)
Email 6 — Day 21:  The Direct Ask (straightforward CTA)
Email 7 — Day 28:  The Breakup (final touch, creates urgency)

TIMING RATIONALE:
- Early emails: 2-3 days apart (front-load while you're fresh in their mind)
- Middle emails: 4-5 days apart (give them breathing room)
- Later emails: 5-7 days apart (lower frequency to avoid annoyance)
```

### Sequence Variations by Goal

```
FOR MEETING BOOKING (aggressive):
5 emails over 14-18 days — faster cadence, stronger CTAs

FOR RELATIONSHIP BUILDING (softer):
6-7 emails over 28-35 days — more value drops, softer CTAs

FOR EVENT FOLLOW-UP:
3-4 emails over 7-10 days — leverage recency, faster follow-up

FOR RE-ENGAGEMENT:
3 emails over 21 days — wide spacing, new angles only
```

## Step 5: Plan Personalization Strategy

### The Three Tiers of Personalization

```
TIER 1: Fully Custom (1-to-1)
──────────────────────────────
Time per email: 5-10 minutes
Volume: 10-20 per day
What's custom: Entire email written for this specific person
When to use: Enterprise accounts, $50K+ deal sizes, C-suite targets
Expected performance: 15-25% reply rate

Example:
"Hi [NAME], I read your recent interview in [PUBLICATION] where you mentioned
[SPECIFIC QUOTE ABOUT THEIR CHALLENGE]. We helped [SIMILAR COMPANY] tackle
the exact same issue and [SPECIFIC RESULT]. Would love to share how — got
15 minutes this week?"

TIER 2: Semi-Personalized (custom first line + template body)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Time per email: 30-60 seconds
Volume: 50-100 per day
What's custom: First 1-2 sentences are researched and personalized; body is templated
When to use: Mid-market, $10K-$50K deal sizes, Director/VP level
Expected performance: 8-15% reply rate

Example:
"Hi [NAME], [CUSTOM FIRST LINE about their company/role/recent news].
[TEMPLATE BODY about the problem, your solution, and a result].
[TEMPLATE CTA]."

Research sources for custom first lines:
- Their LinkedIn activity (posts, comments, job changes)
- Company news (press releases, funding, product launches)
- Their company website (recent blog posts, case studies, team page)
- Industry news that affects their company specifically
- Podcast appearances, webinar participation, conference speaking

TIER 3: Templated (merge fields only)
──────────────────────────────────────
Time per email: 0 seconds (automated)
Volume: 200-500 per day
What's custom: Only merge fields — {{first_name}}, {{company}}, {{title}}, {{industry}}
When to use: High-volume prospecting, <$10K deal sizes, testing new markets
Expected performance: 3-8% reply rate

Example:
"Hi {{first_name}}, most {{title}}s at {{industry}} companies
tell us that [COMMON PAIN POINT] is their biggest bottleneck.
We help with [SOLUTION] — [SIMILAR COMPANY] saw [RESULT].
Worth a quick chat?"

IMPORTANT: Even Tier 3 emails need high-quality templates. "Templated"
does not mean "low effort." The template itself should be excellent.
```

## Step 6: Volume Planning

### Daily Send Calculations

```
FORMULA:
Desired monthly meetings × (100 / positive reply rate) × (100 / reply-to-meeting rate)
= Total emails needed per month

EXAMPLE:
Goal: 10 meetings per month
Positive reply rate: 5%
Reply-to-meeting conversion: 50%

10 × (100/5) × (100/50) = 10 × 20 × 2 = 400 prospects per month
400 prospects × 5 emails per sequence = 2,000 total emails per month
2,000 / 22 working days = ~91 emails per day

INBOX PLANNING:
At 40-50 emails per inbox per day, you need 2-3 active inboxes
to handle 91 emails per day safely.
```

### Volume Staggering

```
- Never send all daily emails in one batch
- Spread sends across the day: morning (8-10 AM), midday (12-1 PM), afternoon (3-4 PM)
- Send in the prospect's time zone (not yours)
- Randomize send times slightly (±15 minutes) to avoid robotic patterns
- Monday morning and Friday afternoon tend to underperform — front-load Tue-Thu
```

## Step 7: A/B Testing Plan

### Build Testing Into Campaign Design from Day 1

```
WEEK 1-2: Test subject lines
- Create 2 subject line variants for Email 1
- Split your list 50/50
- Winner = higher open rate after 100+ sends per variant

WEEK 3-4: Test opening lines
- Use winning subject line from Week 1-2
- Create 2 opening line variants
- Winner = higher reply rate

WEEK 5-6: Test CTAs
- Use winning subject + opener
- Create 2 CTA variants
- Winner = higher meeting booking rate

ONGOING: Continuously test one element at a time
```

## Step 8: Lead Source Strategy

### Where to Build Your Email Lists

```
PRIMARY SOURCES:

1. LinkedIn Sales Navigator → Export to CSV → Enrich with email
   - Best for: Targeted B2B lists based on title, company, industry
   - Tools to enrich: Apollo, Hunter, Snov.io, Lusha, RocketReach

2. Apollo.io
   - Built-in database with email addresses
   - Best for: All-in-one prospecting + email finding
   - Data quality: Generally good, always verify separately

3. ZoomInfo
   - Enterprise-grade B2B database
   - Best for: Large-scale prospecting, technographic data
   - Cost: Higher, but data quality is strong

4. LinkedIn Standard Search + Email Finder Tools
   - Use LinkedIn search to identify prospects
   - Use Hunter.io, Snov.io, or Lusha to find their email
   - More manual but very targeted

5. Industry Directories and Databases
   - Trade association member directories
   - Conference attendee/speaker lists (publicly available)
   - Industry-specific databases (e.g., Crunchbase for startups)

SECONDARY SOURCES:

6. Website Visitor Identification
   - Tools: Clearbit Reveal, Leadfeeder, RB2B
   - Identifies companies (and sometimes individuals) visiting your website
   - Warm leads — they already showed interest

7. Job Board Mining
   - Companies posting jobs relevant to your service area
   - Indicates budget, need, and urgency
   - Example: A company hiring 5 SDRs probably needs sales tools/training

8. Event and Webinar Attendees
   - From events you hosted or attended
   - Higher intent — they engaged with relevant content

9. Content Engagement
   - People who downloaded your resources, commented on your posts, engaged with your content
   - Warm leads with demonstrated interest

10. Referral and Introduction Requests
    - Ask current clients and contacts for introductions
    - Highest conversion rate of any source
```

### Email Verification — Non-Negotiable

```
ALWAYS verify email addresses before adding to any campaign.

Tools:
- NeverBounce (batch verification, good accuracy)
- ZeroBounce (batch + real-time, excellent accuracy)
- MillionVerifier (budget-friendly, decent accuracy)
- Bouncer (European-friendly, GDPR-aware)
- BriteVerify (Validity) (enterprise-grade)

Process:
1. Export your list to CSV
2. Upload to verification tool
3. Only keep addresses marked "valid"
4. Remove: invalid, catch-all, disposable, unknown, role-based
5. Expected valid rate: 70-85% of raw list (if lower, your source is poor)

Cost: $3-10 per 1,000 verifications — this is the cheapest insurance in email marketing.
Skipping this step can destroy your domain reputation in days.
```

### Naming System

```
FORMAT: [ICP-Segment]_[Offer]_[Angle]_[Date]_[Version]

EXAMPLES:
- VP-Marketing_SaaS-50-200_AuditOffer_ProblemAngle_2025-Q1_v1
- CEO_Agency_DemoOffer_ResultAngle_2025-Mar_v2
- Dir-Sales_Midmarket_ConsultOffer_TriggerEvent_2025-Q2_v1

WHY THIS MATTERS:
- You'll eventually have 10-20+ campaigns
- Without consistent naming, you can't find, compare, or learn from past campaigns
- Version numbers track iterations as you optimize
- Date stamps show when campaigns were active
```

## Step 10: Campaign Launch Checklist

### 15-Point Pre-Launch Checklist

```
DELIVERABILITY:
□ 1. SPF, DKIM, and DMARC all verified and passing
□ 2. Custom tracking domain set up and working
□ 3. Domain/inbox has been warmed up (or is established with good reputation)
□ 4. Sending volume is within safe limits for this inbox
□ 5. No blacklist hits on domain or sending IP

LIST QUALITY:
□ 6. All email addresses verified (bounce rate projected <2%)
□ 7. List is segmented — one ICP segment per campaign
□ 8. Role-based addresses (info@, admin@) removed
□ 9. Duplicates removed
□ 10. No emails from competitors or existing clients in the list

CONTENT:
□ 11. All emails proofread — no typos, broken merge fields, or formatting issues
□ 12. Merge fields tested with sample data ({{first_name}} renders correctly)
□ 13. Subject lines are under 50 characters, no spam trigger words
□ 14. Every email has exactly ONE clear CTA
□ 15. A/B test variants are set up for at least the first email's subject line
```

## Step 11: Campaign Monitoring

### Daily Checks (first 7 days)

```
- Open rate by email: Is it above 40%? If below 30%, subject line or deliverability issue.
- Bounce rate: Is it below 2%? If spiking, pause and clean list.
- Reply rate: Any replies coming in? Categorize them (positive, objection, not interested).
- Spam complaints: Any? Even 1-2 spam complaints in the first 100 sends is a red flag.
- Unsubscribe rate: Normal is <1%. Higher = targeting or messaging issue.
```

### Weekly Checks

```
- Total emails sent vs. plan
- Cumulative open rate, reply rate, positive reply rate
- Meeting/demo conversion rate from positive replies
- A/B test progress — enough data to pick a winner?
- Sequence drop-off — where are people going cold? (After email 1? Email 3?)
- Compare performance to benchmarks (see below)
```

### When to Pause or Adjust

```
PAUSE IMMEDIATELY if:
- Bounce rate exceeds 5%
- Spam complaint rate exceeds 0.3%
- Open rate drops below 15% (likely a deliverability issue)
- You receive a blacklist notification

ADJUST if:
- Open rate is 20-35% (test new subject lines)
- Reply rate is below 2% (test new body copy or angle)
- Positive reply rate is below 1% (re-evaluate ICP fit or offer)
- Meeting conversion from replies is below 30% (improve reply handling)
```

## Campaign Blueprint 1: New Market Entry

```
SCENARIO: You're entering a new vertical/market with your existing service.
You have limited social proof in this specific industry.

Segment: [ICP TITLE] at [INDUSTRY] companies, [SIZE RANGE]
Offer: Free strategy session / audit to demonstrate expertise
Angle: Insight angle — show knowledge of their industry challenges
Personalization: Tier 2 (custom first line + templated body)
Volume: 50-75 prospects per week
Sequence: 5 emails over 18 days

Email 1 (Day 1) — The Industry-Specific Opener:
Subject: "[INDUSTRY] and [SPECIFIC CHALLENGE] — quick thought"
Body: Personalized first line + observation about industry trend +
      how you've helped companies in adjacent industries + soft CTA

Email 2 (Day 3) — The Resource Share:
Subject: "resource for [COMPANY]'s [CHALLENGE]"
Body: Share a relevant data point, article, or framework +
      brief tie to how you help + no hard sell

Email 3 (Day 7) — The Adjacent Proof:
Subject: "how [ADJACENT INDUSTRY COMPANY] solved [PROBLEM]"
Body: Case study from a similar (not identical) industry +
      draw parallels to their situation + CTA for a call

Email 4 (Day 12) — The Question:
Subject: "question about [COMPANY]'s [SPECIFIC AREA]"
Body: Ask a thought-provoking question about their strategy +
      hint at a better approach + offer to discuss

Email 5 (Day 18) — The Honest Close:
Subject: "[NAME] — should I close the loop?"
Body: Direct, brief, acknowledge you've reached out before +
      clear final CTA + graceful out if not interested
```

## Campaign Blueprint 2: Warm List Re-Activation

```
SCENARIO: You have a list of old leads, past webinar attendees, or
contacts who downloaded content but never converted.

Segment: Previously engaged contacts from [SOURCE]
Offer: New result / case study / updated offer
Angle: Trigger event (your new development) + existing relationship
Personalization: Tier 2-3 (they already know you, less research needed)
Volume: 100-150 prospects per week
Sequence: 4 emails over 14 days

Email 1 (Day 1) — The Re-Introduction:
Subject: "update since we last connected"
Body: Reference previous interaction + share genuine new development
      (new case study, product update, team expansion) +
      reconnect CTA

Email 2 (Day 4) — The New Result:
Subject: "[X]% [IMPROVEMENT] for [SIMILAR COMPANY]"
Body: Lead with a specific new result you've achieved +
      tie back to what you discussed/they were interested in +
      meeting CTA

Email 3 (Day 8) — The Value Add:
Subject: "thought of you when I saw this"
Body: Share a genuinely useful resource (not your content — industry
      report, competitive insight, relevant article) +
      brief check-in CTA

Email 4 (Day 14) — The Direct Ask:
Subject: "still relevant, [NAME]?"
Body: Straightforward — is [CHALLENGE] still on their radar? +
      Clear yes/no CTA + offer to reconnect or close the file
```

## Campaign Blueprint 3: Event Follow-Up

```
SCENARIO: You attended or hosted an event (conference, webinar,
networking event) and have a list of attendees/contacts.

Segment: Attendees from [EVENT NAME]
Offer: Continue the conversation + specific next step
Angle: Shared experience + mutual connection through the event
Personalization: Tier 1-2 (reference specific event moments)
Volume: Send within 48 hours of the event, entire list
Sequence: 3 emails over 7 days (speed matters for event follow-up)

Email 1 (Day 1 — within 48 hours of event) — The Warm Connect:
Subject: "great connecting at [EVENT]"
Body: Reference a specific session, speaker, or conversation +
      connect it to a relevant challenge they might have +
      offer to continue the conversation

Email 2 (Day 3) — The Value Follow-Up:
Subject: "the [TOPIC] resource I mentioned"
Body: Deliver on something discussed or share a relevant resource
      from the event + add your own insight + soft CTA

Email 3 (Day 7) — The Next Step:
Subject: "next step from [EVENT]?"
Body: Direct — you enjoyed connecting, you think there's an opportunity
      to help with [SPECIFIC THING], would they like to explore it +
      calendar link
```

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing the campaign plan, verify:

- [ ] Campaign follows the "One Campaign, One ICP, One Offer" rule
- [ ] A single, clear goal is defined with a measurable success metric
- [ ] ICP segment is specific enough to enable relevant messaging
- [ ] One of the 8 campaign angles is selected and justified
- [ ] Sequence has 5-7 emails with appropriate timing between each
- [ ] Every email in the sequence has a distinct angle (no "just bumping this up")
- [ ] Personalization tier is selected and is realistic for the volume planned
- [ ] Daily send volume is within safe limits for the number of inboxes
- [ ] A/B testing is planned from day 1
- [ ] Lead source is identified and email verification is planned
- [ ] The 15-point launch checklist is referenced
- [ ] Monitoring plan includes daily and weekly check cadence
- [ ] Pause/adjust triggers are defined
- [ ] Campaign naming follows the convention
- [ ] The plan is complete enough that someone else could execute it without additional context
