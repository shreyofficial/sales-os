# Follow-Up Cadence Master

## Purpose

Build post-interaction follow-up sequences for every scenario you will encounter in outreach. Every conversation — whether it ends in "yes," "not now," "maybe," or silence — requires a specific, value-driven follow-up cadence. This skill gives you pre-built playbooks for 10 common scenarios so you never wonder "What do I say next?" again.

---

## The Follow-Up Philosophy

### Three Truths About Follow-Up

**Truth 1: Every interaction deserves a follow-up.**
If someone accepted your connection, replied to your email, attended your webinar, or was introduced by a mutual contact — they've given you a signal. Not following up is leaving money on the table. Most salespeople quit after one or two attempts. The ones who win follow up five, six, seven times.

**Truth 2: The fortune is in the follow-up.**
Research consistently shows that 80% of sales require at least 5 follow-up contacts. Yet 44% of salespeople give up after one follow-up. The gap between "most salespeople" and "top salespeople" is almost entirely persistence.

**Truth 3: Follow-up must add value.**
"Just checking in" is the laziest, most ignored phrase in sales. Every follow-up message must contain something useful — an insight, a resource, a question, an update, a new angle. The prospect should think "This person keeps giving me useful things" rather than "This person keeps pestering me."

### The Value-First Follow-Up Principle

Before sending any follow-up, ask yourself: **"If I received this message, would I find it valuable — or annoying?"**

Every follow-up should include at least ONE of these value elements:
- A relevant insight or observation about their business, industry, or role
- A resource (article, tool, framework, video) related to their challenges
- A question that makes them think or shows you understand their situation
- An update on something relevant (new case study, industry trend, product improvement)
- A connection or introduction that could benefit them
- A specific idea tailored to their company

**Never send a follow-up that says only:** "Just following up," "Circling back," "Wanted to touch base," "Any thoughts on my last message?" These are not follow-ups — they are reminders, and they signal that YOU need something from THEM rather than the other way around.

### Scenario 1: After Connection Acceptance (LinkedIn)

**Context:** They accepted your LinkedIn connection request. You're now connected. This is a positive signal — they looked at your profile and decided you were worth connecting with. But it does NOT mean they're ready for a pitch.

**Goal of this cadence:** Start a genuine conversation that leads to them understanding what you do and expressing interest.

**Channel:** LinkedIn DM

**Tone:** Warm, peer-to-peer, conversational. No formal language. No pitching in Message 1.

**Message 1 — Sent within 24 hours of acceptance:**
```
Hey [FIRST NAME] — thanks for connecting! I noticed [SPECIFIC
OBSERVATION: their recent post, a project their company launched,
something from their profile headline].

Really interesting stuff. How long have you been focused on [TOPIC
AREA]?
```

**Why this works:** Opens with gratitude, shows you looked at their profile, asks a simple question that's easy to answer. No pitch, no ask.

**Message 2 — Sent 3 days after Message 1 (if no reply):**
```
[FIRST NAME] — I was reading about [INDUSTRY TREND or NEWS
RELEVANT TO THEM] and thought of you. [ONE SENTENCE about why it's
relevant to their role/company].

Curious — is that something your team is seeing too?
```

**Why this works:** Adds value (relevant insight), shows you're thinking about their world, asks a light question. Still no pitch.

**Message 3 — Sent 5 days after Message 2 (if no reply):**
```
Hey [FIRST NAME] — one more thought and then I'll stop clogging your
DMs. I work with [ICP TITLE]s at [COMPANY TYPE] companies on
[SPECIFIC OUTCOME — not your service name, but the result they get].

Recently helped a [SIMILAR COMPANY DESCRIPTION] [SPECIFIC RESULT].

If that's relevant to what you're working on, happy to share how
they did it. If not, no worries — glad to be connected either way.
```

**Why this works:** Transparent about what you do, leads with outcome not features, low-pressure opt-in ("if relevant"), graceful close ("glad to be connected either way").

**If they reply at any point:** Drop the cadence, respond naturally, and guide the conversation toward a discovery call when appropriate.

**Timing summary:** Day 1 > Day 4 > Day 9 (3 messages over ~9 days)

### Scenario 2: After a Positive Email Reply

**Context:** They replied to one of your emails with something positive — "Sounds interesting," "Tell me more," "How does it work?" This is a strong buying signal. Speed matters now.

**Goal of this cadence:** Convert their interest into a booked call within 2-3 exchanges.

**Channel:** Email (stay in the thread they replied to)

**Tone:** Enthusiastic but not desperate. Professional, efficient. Respect their time.

**Message 1 — Reply within 2 hours (ideally faster):**
```
Subject: Re: [ORIGINAL THREAD]

Great to hear from you, [FIRST NAME].

Rather than going back and forth over email, the fastest way to see
if there's a genuine fit would be a quick 15-minute call. I can share
how we've helped [SIMILAR COMPANY TYPE] [ACHIEVE RESULT], and learn a
bit about what [COMPANY] is working on.

Would [DAY/TIME OPTION 1] or [DAY/TIME OPTION 2] work?

(Or here's my calendar link if easier: [LINK])

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Message 2 — Sent 2 days later (if no reply to the call request):**
```
Subject: Re: [ORIGINAL THREAD]

Hey [FIRST NAME] — just floating this to the top of your inbox.
I know things get busy.

Quick 15-min call — I'd share how [SIMILAR COMPANY] went from
[BEFORE STATE] to [AFTER STATE] and see if anything similar applies
to [COMPANY].

Any time this week work? [CALENDAR LINK]

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Message 3 — Sent 3 days after Message 2 (if no reply):**
```
Subject: Re: [ORIGINAL THREAD]

Hi [FIRST NAME] — I know timing can be tricky, so here's a thought:
I put together a quick [2-MINUTE VIDEO / ONE-PAGER / BRIEF SUMMARY]
showing exactly how this works for [COMPANY TYPE] companies.

[LINK TO RESOURCE]

If it resonates, happy to chat. If not, no harm done.

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Message 4 — Sent 5 days after Message 3 (if no reply):**
```
Subject: Re: [ORIGINAL THREAD]

[FIRST NAME] — I know you mentioned interest earlier, and I don't
want to assume you've changed your mind. Priorities shift — totally
get it.

If now isn't the right time, I'm happy to check back in [TIMEFRAME:
a month, next quarter]. Just let me know and I'll set a reminder.

Either way, wishing you and the [COMPANY] team a strong [QUARTER/MONTH].

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Timing summary:** Within 2 hours > Day 3 > Day 6 > Day 11

### Scenario 3: After a "Not Now" Reply

**Context:** They replied with something like: "Interesting but not the right time," "We're focused on other priorities right now," "Maybe later this quarter," "Check back in a few months."

**Goal of this cadence:** Gracefully accept the timing, stay top of mind, and re-engage when the timing is right.

**Channel:** Email (primary), with occasional LinkedIn touchpoints

**Tone:** Respectful, patient, zero pressure. You're playing the long game.

**Message 1 — Immediate reply (same day):**
```
Subject: Re: [ORIGINAL THREAD]

Totally understand, [FIRST NAME] — timing is everything. I appreciate
you letting me know rather than going radio silent.

I'll set a reminder to check back in [TIMEFRAME THEY MENTIONED, or
default to 6-8 weeks]. In the meantime, I'll occasionally share
relevant insights about [THEIR INDUSTRY/CHALLENGE] — feel free to
ignore or engage as you see fit.

Wishing you a great [MONTH/QUARTER].

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Message 2 — Sent 3-4 weeks later (value add):**
```
Subject: Thought of you — [RELEVANT TOPIC]

Hi [FIRST NAME] — came across this [ARTICLE/REPORT/DATA POINT] about
[TOPIC RELEVANT TO THEIR CHALLENGE] and your name came to mind.

[LINK or KEY INSIGHT from the resource]

The part about [SPECIFIC TAKEAWAY] seemed especially relevant for
[COMPANY TYPE] companies. No agenda — just thought you'd find it
interesting.

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Message 3 — Sent at the timeframe they specified (re-engagement):**
```
Subject: Circling back as promised — [TOPIC]

Hi [FIRST NAME] — you mentioned [TIMEFRAME AGO] that the timing
wasn't right for [TOPIC]. Wanted to keep my word and check back in.

Since we last talked, we've [NEW CASE STUDY/RESULT/DEVELOPMENT
that's relevant]. [ONE SENTENCE about what changed or what's new].

Has anything shifted on your end? If [PAIN POINT] is on the radar
now, I'd love to pick up where we left off with a quick call.

If the timing still isn't right, no worries — I'll check back
[NEXT TIMEFRAME].

[YOUR NAME]
```

**LinkedIn touchpoint (between Messages 2 and 3):**
- Engage with their content once or twice (thoughtful comments, not just likes)
- This keeps you visible without emailing them

**Timing summary:** Immediate > 3-4 weeks > [Their specified timeframe, or 6-8 weeks]

### Scenario 4: After Sending a Resource

**Context:** You sent a prospect a resource — a case study, guide, checklist, report, or video. Whether they asked for it or you shared it proactively, the next step is to follow up and learn what resonated.

**Goal of this cadence:** Learn if the resource was useful, then transition to a call.

**Channel:** Same channel the resource was shared on

**Tone:** Curious, helpful. Position yourself as the expert behind the resource.

**Message 1 — Sent 2 days after sharing the resource:**
```
Hey [FIRST NAME] — just wanted to check: did you get a chance to
look at the [RESOURCE NAME] I sent over?

If so, curious which part stood out most — I find that [ICP TITLE]s
usually gravitate toward [SECTION A] or [SECTION B] depending on
where they are with [CHALLENGE].
```

**Why this works:** Specific question (not "what did you think?"), demonstrates expertise by predicting their interest, easy to answer.

**Message 2 — Sent 4 days after Message 1 (if no reply):**
```
[FIRST NAME] — in case the [RESOURCE] got buried, here's the key
takeaway in one line:

"[SINGLE MOST VALUABLE INSIGHT from the resource]"

We found this when working with [SIMILAR COMPANY TYPE] — they were
able to [SPECIFIC RESULT] by applying this.

If you want, I can walk through how it would apply specifically to
[COMPANY] on a quick 15-min call. [CALENDAR LINK]
```

**Message 3 — Sent 5 days after Message 2 (if no reply):**
```
Hey [FIRST NAME] — last note on this. I know [RESOURCE TYPE] can
feel like homework, so here's the TL;DR version:

1. [KEY POINT 1]
2. [KEY POINT 2]
3. [KEY POINT 3]

If any of those hit home for [COMPANY], I've got some ideas on how to
act on them. Happy to share on a call — or just reply here and I'll
send more detail via email.

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Timing summary:** Day 2 > Day 6 > Day 11

### Scenario 5: After No-Show on a Call

**Context:** They booked a call, didn't show up, and didn't cancel. This is frustrating — but don't take it personally. Things come up. The key is prompt, gracious follow-up.

**Goal of this cadence:** Rebook the call without making them feel guilty.

**Channel:** The channel the call was booked through + one cross-channel touchpoint

**Tone:** Understanding, zero guilt. Make it easy for them to rebook.

**Message 1 — Sent within 30 minutes of the missed call (Email):**
```
Subject: Missed you on our call — no worries!

Hey [FIRST NAME] — I was on the line for our [TIME] call but didn't
see you join. Totally understand — things come up.

Would any of these work for a reschedule?
  - [OPTION 1]
  - [OPTION 2]
  - [OPTION 3]

Or grab a time here: [CALENDAR LINK]

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Message 2 — Sent 24 hours later (LinkedIn DM if connected, otherwise email):**
```
Hey [FIRST NAME] — just following up on our missed call yesterday.
No worries at all — want to find a new time?

I was planning to walk through [SPECIFIC VALUE: how X company
achieved Y result and how it could apply to COMPANY].

Happy to work around your schedule. What does this week look like?
```

**Message 3 — Sent 3 days after Message 2 (Email):**
```
Subject: Re: Missed you on our call

Hi [FIRST NAME] — last note on this. I know schedules are crazy,
and I genuinely think a 15-minute conversation would be valuable for
both of us.

If this week doesn't work, I'm happy to book something for next
week: [CALENDAR LINK]

If priorities have shifted and now isn't the right time, that's
totally fine too — just let me know and I'll follow up next
[MONTH/QUARTER].

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Timing summary:** Within 30 min > Day 2 > Day 5

### Scenario 6: After a Call That Didn't Convert

**Context:** You had the call, it went okay, but they didn't move forward. Maybe they said "let me think about it," maybe they need to "discuss internally," or maybe they just weren't ready. This requires a long-term nurture approach.

**Goal of this cadence:** Stay relevant over time so that when they ARE ready, you're the first person they think of.

**Channel:** Email (primary) with periodic LinkedIn engagement

**Tone:** Patient, generous, no pressure. You're building a relationship, not chasing a deal.

**Message 1 — Sent 1 day after the call:**
```
Subject: Great chatting, [FIRST NAME]

[FIRST NAME] — really enjoyed our conversation yesterday. A few
things stuck with me:

- [INSIGHT OR POINT THEY MADE during the call]
- [HOW YOUR SOLUTION ADDRESSES their specific situation]

As promised, here's [ANYTHING YOU PROMISED TO SEND: resource, case
study, pricing summary, next steps doc].

Take your time reviewing. Happy to answer any questions when you're
ready.

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Message 2 — Sent 5 days after the call:**
```
Subject: Re: Great chatting, [FIRST NAME]

Hey [FIRST NAME] — wanted to check in and see if you had a chance to
look over [WHAT YOU SENT]. Any questions or thoughts?

Also, I just came across [RELEVANT INSIGHT/ARTICLE/DATA POINT] that
ties into what we discussed about [THEIR SPECIFIC CHALLENGE]. Thought
you'd find it interesting: [LINK]

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Message 3 — Sent 2 weeks after the call:**
```
Subject: Update: [RELEVANT NEWS OR RESULT]

Hi [FIRST NAME] — quick update I thought you'd appreciate:

[NEW CASE STUDY, RESULT, OR DEVELOPMENT that's relevant to their
situation]. [ONE SPECIFIC DATA POINT].

Given what you told me about [THEIR SITUATION], this seemed directly
applicable.

If you'd like to revisit our conversation about [YOUR SERVICE AREA],
I'm here. If the timing still isn't right, no rush — I'll keep
sharing things I think you'll find valuable.

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Message 4 — Sent 4-6 weeks after the call:**
```
Subject: [INDUSTRY TREND] and [COMPANY]

[FIRST NAME] — I've been noticing [INDUSTRY TREND] picking up
steam, especially for [COMPANY TYPE] companies. [1-2 SENTENCES
about the trend].

With [COMPANY]'s focus on [WHAT THEY TOLD YOU], this seems like
it could be relevant.

Thought of our conversation and wanted to flag it. If you ever want
to pick things back up, I'd love to reconnect.

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Message 5 — Sent 3 months after the call:**
```
Subject: It's been a while — how's [COMPANY]?

Hey [FIRST NAME] — it's been a few months since we last chatted, and
I wanted to check in.

How's [SPECIFIC INITIATIVE OR CHALLENGE they mentioned on the call]
going?

We've had some [NEW DEVELOPMENTS: new clients, new approach, new
results] since we last spoke, and I think there might be an even
stronger fit now.

Would it be worth a quick 15-minute call to reconnect? No pressure
either way.

[YOUR NAME]
```

**LinkedIn touchpoints (throughout):**
- Engage with their content every 2-3 weeks (genuine comments)
- Share content they'd find relevant and tag them (sparingly — once per quarter max)

**Timing summary:** Day 1 > Day 5 > Week 2 > Week 5 > Month 3

### Scenario 7: After They Went Silent Mid-Conversation

**Context:** You were in the middle of an active conversation — multiple replies exchanged — and they suddenly stopped responding. No explanation, no "not now," just silence.

**Goal of this cadence:** Re-engage without being needy or guilt-inducing.

**Channel:** Start on the channel they went silent on, then try the other channel.

**Tone:** Light, understanding, slightly humorous. Do NOT reference their silence directly ("I noticed you stopped replying").

**Message 1 — Sent 3 days after their last message (same channel):**
```
Hey [FIRST NAME] — I know inboxes get wild. Picking up where we
left off:

[BRIEF SUMMARY of the last point of conversation — one sentence]

Would [SPECIFIC NEXT STEP you were discussing] still make sense?
Happy to adjust if priorities shifted.
```

**Message 2 — Sent 4 days after Message 1 (switch channels: if email, try LinkedIn DM; if LinkedIn, try email):**
```
Hey [FIRST NAME] — thought I'd try you over here in case the other
channel got buried. We were chatting about [TOPIC] and I had one
more thought:

[NEW VALUE: a relevant insight, a quick idea for their company,
or a resource]

Would love to pick the conversation back up if the timing works.
```

**Message 3 — Sent 5 days after Message 2 (original channel):**
```
[FIRST NAME] — no worries if things got hectic. I'll keep this
simple:

If [TOPIC] is still on your radar, I've got [SPECIFIC VALUE YOU CAN
OFFER] ready to share. Just say the word.

If priorities shifted, totally get it. Happy to reconnect down the
road whenever it makes sense.

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Timing summary:** Day 3 > Day 7 > Day 12 (from their last message)

### Scenario 8: After They Asked to Be Contacted Later

**Context:** They explicitly said something like: "Reach out in Q3," "Let's revisit in January," "Contact me in 2 months." This is a green light with a time delay. Honor their request precisely.

**Goal of this cadence:** Stay on their radar between now and the specified time, then re-engage right on schedule.

**Channel:** Email (primary), with one LinkedIn touchpoint mid-wait

**Tone:** Respectful of their timeline, organized, professional.

**Message 1 — Immediate reply when they give the timeframe:**
```
Subject: Re: [THREAD]

Perfect, [FIRST NAME]. I've got a note in my calendar for [THEIR
SPECIFIED DATE/TIMEFRAME]. I'll reach out then.

In the meantime, if anything changes or if [PAIN POINT] becomes
more urgent, don't hesitate to reach out early. I'm here.

Talk soon.

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Message 2 — Sent midway through the waiting period (value add, not a check-in):**
```
Subject: Quick share — thought of [COMPANY]

Hi [FIRST NAME] — I know we're set to reconnect in [TIMEFRAME],
and I'm not trying to jump the gun. But I came across this and
your name immediately came to mind:

[RELEVANT RESOURCE, ARTICLE, INSIGHT, OR DATA POINT]

The key insight: [ONE SENTENCE]. Figured it might be useful as
you plan for [QUARTER/INITIATIVE they mentioned].

No response needed — just wanted to share. Talk to you in
[REMAINING TIMEFRAME].

[YOUR NAME]
```

**LinkedIn touchpoint (during the waiting period):**
- Engage with their content once or twice — nothing aggressive, just staying visible

**Message 3 — Sent on the specified date (re-engagement):**
```
Subject: As promised — reconnecting about [TOPIC]

Hi [FIRST NAME] — we spoke back in [ORIGINAL MONTH] and you
mentioned [TIMEFRAME] would be better timing for [TOPIC]. Here I am,
right on schedule.

- [NEW DEVELOPMENT 1: new case study, new capability, new result]
- [NEW DEVELOPMENT 2: relevant to their specific situation]

Would it make sense to pick up where we left off with a quick
15-minute call?

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Message 4 — Sent 4 days after Message 3 (if no reply):**
```
Subject: Re: As promised — reconnecting about [TOPIC]

Hey [FIRST NAME] — just floating this up in case it got buried.
I know [TIMEFRAME] was the window you mentioned for revisiting
[TOPIC].

Is this still on your radar? Happy to chat this week or next.

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Timing summary:** Immediate > Midway through wait > On the specified date > 4 days after re-engagement

### Scenario 9: After a Referral Introduction

**Context:** Someone introduced you to a prospect via email or LinkedIn. This is the warmest lead you can get. Don't blow it with a generic pitch.

**Goal of this cadence:** Honor the introduction, build quick rapport, and book a call within the first 2 exchanges.

**Channel:** Same channel the introduction was made on

**Tone:** Grateful, warm, referencing the introducer, confident but not aggressive.

**Message 1 — Sent within 4 hours of the introduction:**
```
Subject: Re: Introduction — [YOUR NAME] <> [PROSPECT NAME]

Hi [PROSPECT NAME] — great to meet you! Thanks [INTRODUCER NAME]
for connecting us.

[INTRODUCER NAME] mentioned that you're [BRIEF CONTEXT of what the
introducer said about why they connected you — "scaling your marketing
team," "looking at new tools for X," "experiencing challenges with Y"].

I've been helping [ICP TITLE]s at [COMPANY TYPE] companies with
exactly that — most recently, [ONE-SENTENCE RESULT with a similar
company].

Would love to learn more about what [COMPANY] is working on and
see if I can be helpful. Do you have 15 minutes this week?

[OPTION 1] or [OPTION 2] work on my end, or feel free to grab a
time: [CALENDAR LINK]

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Why speed matters:** Referral introductions have a short shelf life. The prospect is thinking about you RIGHT NOW because the introducer just mentioned you. Within 24 hours, you're a fading memory. Within 48, you're forgotten.

**Message 2 — Sent 3 days after Message 1 (if no reply):**
```
Subject: Re: Introduction — [YOUR NAME] <> [PROSPECT NAME]

Hey [PROSPECT NAME] — just bumping this up. I know [INTRODUCER NAME]
thought we'd have a good conversation, and I'd hate to let the
intro go to waste.

Happy to keep it quick — 15 minutes to swap notes and see if there's
a fit. If this isn't the right time, no worries — just let me know
and I'll follow up later.

[CALENDAR LINK]

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Message 3 — Sent 5 days after Message 2 (if no reply):**
```
Subject: Re: Introduction — [YOUR NAME] <> [PROSPECT NAME]

Hi [PROSPECT NAME] — last note from me on this. I know things get
busy, and I don't want to be the persistent stranger in your inbox.

If [TOPIC/CHALLENGE] is something you'd like to explore, I'm
here and happy to chat. If the timing isn't right, that's completely
fine — I'll close the loop with [INTRODUCER NAME].

All the best,

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Important:** After sending Message 3 with no response, let the introducer know: "Hey [INTRODUCER NAME], I reached out to [PROSPECT NAME] a few times but haven't connected yet. No worries at all — appreciate the intro either way." This maintains the relationship with your referral source.

**Timing summary:** Within 4 hours > Day 3 > Day 8

**Goal of this cadence:** Capitalize on the momentum of the shared experience before it fades.

**Channel:** Email first (you likely collected their email at the event), then LinkedIn

**Tone:** Energetic, referencing the shared experience, capitalizing on the "event high."

**Message 1 — Sent within 24 hours of the event:**

## Follow-Up Automation vs. Personal Attention

### What Can Be Automated

| Scenario | Automatable? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Post-connection acceptance (Scenario 1) | Partially | Message 1 can be templated; Messages 2-3 should be lightly personalized |
| After "not now" — nurture drip (Scenario 3) | Yes | Long-term value drips can be scheduled in advance |
| After sending a resource (Scenario 4) | Partially | Messages 1-2 can be templated; Message 3 should reference specific resource sections |
| Post-event sequence (Scenario 10) | Partially | First message should be personal; follow-ups can be templated |

### What Needs Personal Attention

| Scenario | Why Automation Fails |
|---|---|
| After positive email reply (Scenario 2) | They said something specific — your reply must address it |
| After no-show (Scenario 5) | Requires empathy and real-time context |
| After a call that didn't convert (Scenario 6) | References specific call topics, promises made, next steps discussed |
| After they went silent mid-conversation (Scenario 7) | Must reference the specific conversation thread |
| After a referral introduction (Scenario 9) | The introducer's name and context must be honored |
| After they asked to be contacted later (Scenario 8) | Timing is specific; interim messages need personal relevance |

### Practical Automation Setup

For scenarios that can be partially automated:
1. Write template messages with [BRACKETS] for personalization fields
2. Schedule the cadence in your email tool (Instantly, Lemlist, or even Gmail's "Schedule Send")
3. Before each message sends, spend 2-3 minutes filling in personalization fields
4. For LinkedIn DMs: use reminders (Google Calendar, task manager) since most DM tools are risky for LinkedIn ToS compliance

## Follow-Up Tracking

### Managing Dozens of Active Cadences Without Dropping Balls

When you're running 30-60 omnichannel sequences AND managing follow-ups for 10 different scenarios, things slip through the cracks. Here's how to prevent that.

**Method 1: The Follow-Up Queue (Spreadsheet)**

Add a tab to your pipeline tracker called "Follow-Up Queue":

| Prospect | Scenario | Last Message Date | Next Message | Next Message Date | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sarah Chen | #2 - Positive Reply | 2026-04-01 | Message 2 — Call request bump | 2026-04-03 | Active |
| Mike Torres | #5 - No-Show | 2026-04-02 | Message 2 — Cross-channel DM | 2026-04-03 | Active |

## The 3-Strike Rule

### After 3 Value-Add Follow-Ups With No Response

When you've sent 3 follow-up messages (each containing genuine value) and received zero response, it's time to shift strategies — NOT give up entirely.

**What to do:**

1. **Send a final "open door" message:**
```
[FIRST NAME] — I've shared a few things I thought might be useful
and haven't heard back, which is totally fine. I'll stop reaching
out for now.

If [PAIN POINT/TOPIC] ever becomes a priority, feel free to reply
to any of these messages — I'll be here.

Wishing you and [COMPANY] all the best.

[YOUR NAME]
```

2. **Move to long-term nurture:**
- Add them to your monthly or quarterly content share list
- Continue to engage with their LinkedIn content (passively — likes, occasional comments)
- Set a calendar reminder to re-evaluate in 90 days

3. **Do NOT delete them from your system:**
- People change jobs, priorities shift, budgets open up, competitors disappoint them
- A prospect who ignored you 6 months ago might book a call tomorrow after a triggering event
- Keep their record with full history so you can reference previous conversations when re-engaging

4. **Analyze the pattern:**
- If many leads are hitting the 3-strike rule, the problem is upstream (your targeting, messaging, or value proposition)
- If it's occasional, it's normal — not everyone is a buyer right now

## Quality Checklist

Before deploying your follow-up cadences, verify:

- [ ] Every follow-up message contains genuine value (insight, resource, question, or update) — not just a "checking in"
- [ ] Timing between messages is appropriate for each scenario (urgent scenarios = faster cadence; nurture scenarios = slower cadence)
- [ ] Channel choices match where the conversation started (don't switch randomly)
- [ ] Tone matches the scenario (empathetic for no-shows, energetic for post-event, patient for "not now")
- [ ] The 3-strike rule is built into every cadence — you know when to stop active outreach
- [ ] You have a tracking system (spreadsheet, CRM tasks, or calendar) set up to manage active follow-ups
- [ ] Each cadence has clear exit criteria — you know when a prospect moves to long-term nurture
- [ ] Templates are personalized to your business — no generic placeholders left in the messages you'll actually send
- [ ] You've pressure-tested each cadence by reading all messages in sequence from the prospect's perspective — does it feel respectful and valuable, or repetitive and annoying?
- [ ] Follow-up cadences align with your pipeline stages — you know which stage a prospect is in at each step
- [ ] You've identified which cadences apply to your current active prospects and queued up the first messages
