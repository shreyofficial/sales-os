# Weekly Pipeline Review Generator

## Purpose

Generate a structured weekly performance report that turns raw outreach data into actionable insights. Instead of guessing how your pipeline is performing, you fill in the numbers, and this framework tells you what is working, what is broken, where to focus, and what to change. Run this every week to catch problems early, compound your wins, and continuously optimize your sales system.

---

## Why Weekly Reviews Matter

### Without Weekly Reviews

- You run outreach for a month, book 2 meetings, and have no idea why it wasn't 10
- You don't notice that your email open rate dropped 40% because your domain reputation took a hit
- Your best lead went silent 2 weeks ago and you forgot about them
- You keep doing the same thing expecting different results because you have no feedback loop
- You feel "busy" but can't point to improvement

### With Weekly Reviews

- You catch a declining open rate in week 2 and fix it before it tanks
- You notice that LinkedIn DMs are outperforming email and shift your effort accordingly
- You see that 80% of your pipeline is stuck in "Engaged" and rewrite your CTAs
- You identify your top 3 leads and craft personalized follow-ups before anyone else does
- You know exactly what to improve next week — one change, high impact

### The Compound Effect

Small weekly adjustments compound dramatically over time:
- Week 1: Fix subject lines → open rates increase 15%
- Week 2: Rewrite CTAs → reply rates increase 10%
- Week 3: Add social proof earlier → conversation rates increase 20%
- Week 4: Tighten follow-up timing → 3 more meetings booked

After one month of weekly optimization, your pipeline could be 2-3x more efficient than when you started. But ONLY if you're measuring, reviewing, and adjusting weekly.

## The Weekly Pipeline Review Framework

### Section 1: Volume Metrics

**What to measure:** How much outreach activity are you generating?

| Metric | This Week | Last Week | Target | Trend |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| New prospects identified | ___ | ___ | ___ | Up/Down/Flat |
| First touches sent (Email) | ___ | ___ | ___ | Up/Down/Flat |
| First touches sent (LinkedIn) | ___ | ___ | ___ | Up/Down/Flat |
| Total outreach messages sent (all channels, all sequence steps) | ___ | ___ | ___ | Up/Down/Flat |
| LinkedIn connection requests sent | ___ | ___ | ___ | Up/Down/Flat |
| Content engagements (comments, likes on prospect posts) | ___ | ___ | ___ | Up/Down/Flat |

**How to fill this in:**
- Email sends: Check your email tool (Instantly, Lemlist, Smartlead) or Gmail sent folder
- LinkedIn activity: Check LinkedIn's "My Network" page for pending/sent requests, and your DM history for messages sent
- Content engagements: Count the comments and likes you left on prospect posts this week

**Key questions to ask:**
- Am I hitting my weekly volume targets from the Pipeline Capacity Model?
- If volume is down, why? (Time constraints? Running out of prospects? Technical issues?)
- If volume is up, is quality keeping pace? (More messages is only good if they're still personalized)

**Red flags:**
- Volume dropped more than 20% week over week without explanation
- You sent zero LinkedIn messages (single-channel risk)
- You identified zero new prospects (your pipeline will dry up in 2-3 weeks)

### Section 2: Engagement Metrics

**What to measure:** How are prospects responding to your outreach?

| Metric | This Week | Last Week | Benchmark | Trend |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Email open rate | ___% | ___% | 40-60% | Up/Down/Flat |
| Email reply rate | ___% | ___% | 3-8% | Up/Down/Flat |
| Email positive reply rate | ___% | ___% | 2-5% | Up/Down/Flat |
| Email bounce rate | ___% | ___% | <2% | Up/Down/Flat |
| Email unsubscribe rate | ___% | ___% | <1% | Up/Down/Flat |
| LinkedIn connection acceptance rate | ___% | ___% | 20-40% | Up/Down/Flat |
| LinkedIn DM response rate | ___% | ___% | 10-25% | Up/Down/Flat |
| LinkedIn profile views received | ___ | ___ | — | Up/Down/Flat |
| Link click rate (emails) | ___% | ___% | 2-5% | Up/Down/Flat |

**How to fill this in:**
- Email metrics: Your email sequencing tool dashboard (most tools show open, reply, bounce, and click rates)
- LinkedIn acceptance: (Acceptances this week / Requests sent in the last 7-14 days) x 100
- LinkedIn DM response: (DM replies received / DMs sent this week) x 100
- Profile views: LinkedIn "Who viewed your profile" section

**Industry Benchmarks for Comparison:**

| Metric | Poor | Average | Good | Excellent |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Email open rate | <25% | 25-40% | 40-60% | >60% |
| Email reply rate | <1% | 1-3% | 3-8% | >8% |
| Email bounce rate | >5% | 2-5% | 1-2% | <1% |
| LI acceptance rate | <10% | 10-20% | 20-40% | >40% |
| LI DM response rate | <5% | 5-10% | 10-25% | >25% |

**Key questions to ask:**
- Are engagement metrics trending up, down, or flat?
- Which channel is performing better — email or LinkedIn? (Allocate more effort to the winner)
- If open rates dropped: Is it a subject line issue, a deliverability issue, or a list quality issue?
- If reply rates dropped but open rates are fine: The email body or CTA needs work
- If bounce rates spiked: List quality issue — verify emails before sending
- If unsubscribes spiked: Messaging is off-target or frequency is too high

**Red flags:**
- Open rate below 25% (deliverability problem — check spam folder, warm up domain, reduce volume)
- Reply rate below 1% for more than 2 weeks (messaging is fundamentally off — overhaul the email copy)
- Bounce rate above 5% (stop sending immediately, verify your list, remove invalid emails)
- LinkedIn acceptance below 10% (profile needs optimization or connection notes are too salesy)

### Section 3: Conversion Metrics

**What to measure:** How effectively are you turning outreach into meetings?

| Metric | This Week | Last Week | Target | Trend |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conversations started | ___ | ___ | ___ | Up/Down/Flat |
| Meetings booked | ___ | ___ | ___ | Up/Down/Flat |
| Meetings completed | ___ | ___ | ___ | Up/Down/Flat |
| No-shows | ___ | ___ | ___ | Up/Down/Flat |
| Outreach-to-meeting rate | ___% | ___% | 2-5% | Up/Down/Flat |
| Conversation-to-meeting rate | ___% | ___% | 25-50% | Up/Down/Flat |
| No-show rate | ___% | ___% | <25% | Up/Down/Flat |

**How to fill this in:**
- Conversations started: Count prospects who replied and engaged in a back-and-forth this week
- Meetings booked: Count new calendar events with prospects
- Meetings completed: Count calls that actually happened
- No-shows: Count calls that were booked but the prospect didn't show
- Outreach-to-meeting rate: (Meetings booked / First touches sent in the last 14-21 days) x 100
- Conversation-to-meeting rate: (Meetings booked / Conversations that were active this week) x 100

**Key questions to ask:**
- Am I on pace to hit my monthly meeting target?
- If meetings are below target: is the problem at the top of the funnel (not enough outreach) or the bottom (not enough conversion)?
- Is my no-show rate acceptable? If above 30%, my confirmation process needs improvement.
- What was the channel source for each booked meeting? (Track which channel — email or LinkedIn — originated each meeting)

**Red flags:**
- Zero meetings booked for 2 consecutive weeks (something fundamental is broken — diagnose urgently)
- No-show rate above 40% (confirmation cadence is failing or meetings are booked too far out)
- High conversation volume but low meeting conversion (your call-to-action or call-booking process needs work)

**Pipeline Shape Analysis:**

Draw your funnel (or just compare the numbers):
```

Identified:        [___]     [|||||||||||||||||||]
First Touch:       [___]     [||||||||||||||||||]
```

**Is your funnel shape healthy?**
- Healthy: Gradual narrowing from top to bottom. Each stage has fewer leads than the one above.
- Unhealthy: A "bulge" in the middle (e.g., lots of "Engaged" leads but very few "Conversations") indicates a bottleneck.
- Unhealthy: Very wide top and very narrow bottom with huge drop-offs indicates a messaging or targeting problem.
- Unhealthy: Very narrow top indicates you're not prospecting enough — your pipeline will dry up.

**Key questions to ask:**
- Where is the biggest drop-off? That's where I need to focus optimization efforts.
- Are any stages growing (leads accumulating) without proportional growth in the next stage? That's a bottleneck.
- Is the pipeline getting bigger or smaller overall? (Shrinking pipeline = future meeting drought)

**Red flags:**

- All campaigns performing similarly poorly — the problem is likely systemic (ICP targeting, value proposition, or deliverability)
- A previously strong campaign suddenly declining — check for burnout (same people seeing your messages in industry circles), deliverability changes, or market shifts

### Setting Up a Quick Data Collection Process

To make the weekly review fast (under 15 minutes), prepare your data BEFORE the review:

**Friday 4:00 PM (or your chosen review time):**
1. Open your email tool dashboard — screenshot or note the weekly stats (2 min)
2. Open LinkedIn — count connection requests sent/accepted, DMs sent/replied this week (3 min)
3. Open your pipeline tracker — refresh stage counts and lead scores (2 min)
4. Open your calendar — count meetings booked, completed, no-shows (2 min)
5. Fill in the review template below (5-10 min)

Total prep + review time: 15-20 minutes. This is the most valuable 15 minutes of your sales week.

**How to interpret trends:**

- **Flat:** You've hit a plateau. Time for a new approach or expanded volume.
- **Volatile (up/down/up/down):** Your process isn't consistent. Standardize your daily routine and messaging.

## Monthly Rollup: From Weekly Tactics to Monthly Strategy

### How Weekly Reviews Feed Into Monthly Reviews

Every 4 weeks, take 30 minutes for a deeper monthly review that synthesizes your weekly data.

**Monthly Review Questions:**

1. **Results vs. Goals:** How many meetings did I book this month vs. target? What was the gap and why?
2. **Best-performing channel:** Email or LinkedIn — which drove more meetings? Should I shift the ratio?
3. **Best-performing campaign:** Which sequence/variant produced the most meetings? Can I scale it?
4. **ICP validation:** Are the people I'm booking meetings with actually matching my ICP? If not, is my targeting off or is my ICP wrong?
5. **Process efficiency:** Am I spending time in the right places? (Compare time spent on A-leads vs. D-leads)
6. **Resource gaps:** Do I need new case studies, resources, or content to strengthen my outreach?
7. **Tool evaluation:** Are my tools (email tool, CRM, LinkedIn) serving me well, or is it time to upgrade?

**Monthly Action Items:**

Based on the monthly review, define:
- One change to ICP or targeting (if needed)
- One change to messaging or sequences (based on performance data)
- One process improvement (time management, tool adoption, tracking)
- Updated monthly targets (based on actual conversion rates, not guesses)

## Example Analysis: What Good Insights Look Like

### Sample Filled-In Review (Excerpt)

```
--- SECTION 2: ENGAGEMENT ---
Email open rate:          52% (benchmark: 40-60%)       ✓ Good
Email reply rate:         2.1% (benchmark: 3-8%)        ✗ Below
LI acceptance rate:       35% (benchmark: 20-40%)       ✓ Good
LI DM response rate:      8% (benchmark: 10-25%)        ~ Borderline
```

### Sample AI Analysis of This Data

"Your open rates are solid (52%), which means your subject lines and deliverability are working. However, your reply rate (2.1%) is below the 3% benchmark despite healthy opens. This tells me the problem is inside the email — specifically your body copy, value proposition, or CTA. People are opening but not compelled to reply.

Three specific changes to test next week:
1. **Shorten your emails to under 80 words.** At 2.1% reply rate with 52% opens, prospects are opening, reading, and deciding it's not worth their time. Shorter emails create less friction.
2. **Switch from a meeting CTA to a question CTA.** Instead of 'Would you be open to a call?', try 'Curious — how is your team currently handling [SPECIFIC CHALLENGE]?' Questions get 40% more replies than meeting requests in cold email.
3. **Lead with a stronger pain point.** Your current opener might be too generic. Test opening with a specific, quantified problem: 'Most [ICP TITLE]s I talk to are losing [X HOURS/DOLLARS] per [TIMEFRAME] on [SPECIFIC PROBLEM].'"

This is what a useful weekly insight looks like — specific, data-driven, and actionable. Not "things look okay, keep going."

## Quality Checklist

Before running your first weekly review, verify:

- [ ] You have data sources set up for every metric in the template (email tool, LinkedIn, CRM/spreadsheet, calendar)
- [ ] You can access all data sources quickly (bookmarked, logged in, shortcuts ready) — the review should take 15 minutes, not 45
- [ ] Your pipeline tracker is current (all leads are in the correct stage with accurate scores)
- [ ] You've scheduled a recurring calendar event for the weekly review (same day, same time, every week)
- [ ] You have a place to store completed reviews so you can reference previous weeks (folder, doc, or spreadsheet tab)
- [ ] You have benchmark numbers filled in (from this document or from your own historical data)
- [ ] You understand how to calculate each metric (formulas are clear, data sources are mapped)
- [ ] You've identified which AI analysis prompts are most relevant to your current situation
- [ ] You've committed to identifying ONE BIG THING every week — not 10 things, ONE thing
- [ ] You have a plan for the monthly rollup (scheduled for the last week of each month)
- [ ] Your first review will be a "baseline" — don't stress about the numbers, just establish where you are so you can measure improvement from here
