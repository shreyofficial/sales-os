# Outreach Automation Planner

## Purpose

Map every sales task in your workflow, decide what to automate and what to keep manual, choose the right tools for your budget, and build an implementation plan that saves you hours per week without losing the human touch that actually books meetings. Automation done right is a force multiplier. Automation done wrong is a reputation destroyer.

---

## The Automation Philosophy

**Automate the repeatable. Personalize the impactful.**

Not everything should be automated. The goal of automation is to eliminate low-value, repetitive tasks so you can spend more time on the high-value, relationship-building activities that actually close deals.

### The Automation Spectrum

Think of every task on a spectrum:

```
FULLY MANUAL ←————————————————————→ FULLY AUTOMATED
                                    
Relationship     Personalized      Semi-automated     Templated        Fully
building,        outreach,         with human         sequences,       automated
objection        first-touch       review/approval    follow-ups,      data entry,
handling,        DMs, call                            scheduling       verification,
negotiation      conversations                                         warm-up
```

**The rule:** Move tasks to the right only as far as quality allows. The moment automation degrades quality noticeably, pull it back one step.

### The Danger of Over-Automation

Signs you've automated too much:
- Prospects reply saying your message feels automated
- LinkedIn sends a warning about automated activity
- Reply rate drops after implementing automation
- You're sending messages you haven't personally reviewed
- Your outreach sounds identical to every other automated message in their inbox
- You can't remember what's being sent on your behalf

Signs you haven't automated enough:
- You spend more than 2 hours/day on data entry and admin tasks
- Follow-ups slip through the cracks regularly
- You manually copy-paste the same message repeatedly
- You lose track of where prospects are in your sequence
- You could be having conversations but you're doing spreadsheet work instead

## The Automation Opportunity Map

Categorize every sales task into one of three buckets:

### AUTOMATE FULLY
These tasks are repetitive, rule-based, and don't require human judgment:

| Task | Why Automate | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| Email sending/scheduling | Same sequence, sent at predetermined times | Use sequencing tool |
| Follow-up reminders | Rule-based: if no reply after X days, trigger next step | Built into most tools |
| Data entry into CRM | Logging emails, updating contact records | CRM integrations |
| List building (initial scrape) | Pulling contacts from LinkedIn, databases | Use prospecting tools |
| Email verification | Bounce checking before sending | Verification API |
| Email warm-up | Automated inbox warming for new domains | Dedicated warm-up tool |
| Basic reporting | Weekly metric summaries | Dashboard/spreadsheet |
| Calendar scheduling | Once prospect agrees to a call | Scheduling link tool |
| Contact enrichment | Finding emails, phone numbers, company data | Enrichment tools |
| Unsubscribe handling | Legal requirement, must be automated | Built into sequencing tools |
| Bounce processing | Auto-removing invalid addresses | Built into sending tools |
| Timezone detection | Sending at right time for prospect | Most sequencing tools handle this |

### AUTOMATE WITH HUMAN REVIEW
These tasks benefit from AI/tools doing the heavy lifting, but need a human eye before executing:

| Task | Automation Role | Human Role |
|------|----------------|------------|
| Email copy generation | AI writes the first draft | Human reviews, edits voice/tone, approves |
| LinkedIn connection requests | Tool queues the sends | Human reviews each profile before sending |
| Reply categorization | AI categorizes as positive/negative/neutral | Human verifies, handles edge cases |
| Lead scoring updates | Tool scores based on engagement data | Human reviews scores before acting |
| Prospect research | Tool pulls company data, recent news | Human synthesizes into personalization |
| Sequence assignment | Tool suggests which sequence fits | Human confirms before enrolling |
| Meeting prep | AI compiles prospect info into brief | Human reviews and adds context |
| Objection response drafts | AI suggests response templates | Human personalizes and sends |
| Campaign performance alerts | Tool flags when metrics cross thresholds | Human investigates and decides action |
| Template refresh suggestions | AI suggests when templates are stale | Human rewrites or approves new versions |

### KEEP MANUAL
These tasks require human judgment, relationship skills, or creative thinking that automation cannot replicate:

| Task | Why Keep Manual |
|------|----------------|
| First-touch DM personalization | Must feel genuinely personal to stand out |
| Call booking conversations | Requires reading the prospect's tone and adapting |
| Objection handling (complex) | Each objection has nuance; canned responses feel dismissive |
| Relationship building | Authentic engagement cannot be faked at scale |
| Content creation for pipeline | Thought leadership must reflect real expertise |
| Discovery calls | Real-time listening and adapting |
| Proposal writing | Must address specific prospect needs |
| Strategic account planning | Requires synthesis of multiple data points |
| Networking and referral conversations | Trust-based, cannot be templated |
| Pricing negotiations | Requires reading the room and business judgment |

## Tool Stack Recommendations by Budget

### $0/Month — Bootstrap Stack

Best for: Just starting out, validating your ICP and messaging before investing in tools.

| Function | Tool | Details |
|----------|------|---------|
| CRM/Tracking | Google Sheets | Create a simple pipeline tracker with columns for: Name, Company, Email, LinkedIn URL, Stage, Last Touch, Next Action, Notes |
| Email sending | Gmail / Outlook | Send emails manually using templates saved in a doc. Cap at 20-30/day to protect your domain. |
| LinkedIn outreach | LinkedIn Free | Manual connection requests and DMs. Limited to ~100 connection requests/week. |
| Copy assistance | Free AI tools | Use free tiers of AI assistants for drafting emails and DMs |
| Email finding | Hunter.io free tier | 25 searches/month. Supplement with LinkedIn profile → company email pattern guessing |
| Scheduling | Google Calendar | Share available times manually, or use Calendly free tier (1 event type) |

**Workflow at this level:**
1. Spend 30 min building a list of 10-15 prospects in Google Sheets
2. Research each prospect (5 min each)
3. Write and send personalized emails (2-3 min each)
4. Track in spreadsheet
5. Set reminders to follow up
6. Total time: 2-3 hours/day for 10-15 prospects

**When to upgrade:** When you've validated that your messaging gets replies and you need to increase volume beyond what manual sending allows (typically after 4-8 weeks of manual outreach).

### $50-150/Month — Starter Stack

Best for: Proven messaging, ready to increase volume while maintaining quality.

| Function | Tool Options | Estimated Cost |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| Email sequencing | Instantly ($30/mo), Smartlead ($39/mo), Lemlist ($59/mo) | $30-59/mo |
| CRM | HubSpot Free, Pipedrive Starter ($15/mo), Close.com ($29/mo) | $0-29/mo |

| Email warm-up | Usually included in sequencing tool | $0 |
| Scheduling | Calendly Free or Cal.com Free | $0 |

**What to look for in an email sequencing tool:**
- Multiple inbox support (send from 3-5 inboxes)
- Built-in warm-up
- A/B testing capability
- Reply detection (pauses sequence when prospect replies)
- Timezone-aware sending
- Basic analytics (open rate, reply rate by campaign)
- Email verification integration

**Questions to ask before buying:**
- Can I connect multiple email accounts?
- Is warm-up included or separate?
- What's the daily sending limit per inbox?
- Can I A/B test subject lines and body copy?
- Does it pause the sequence when someone replies?
- What's the cancellation policy?

**Red flags in tools at this level:**
- No warm-up feature (you'll need a separate tool)
- Limits on the number of prospects/contacts
- No A/B testing
- Poor deliverability reputation (research in community forums)
- Long-term contracts required

**Workflow at this level:**
1. Build lists weekly (batch research)
2. Verify emails before uploading
3. Create sequences in your tool (3-5 emails per sequence)
4. Upload verified lists, assign to sequences
5. Review replies daily, respond to interested prospects manually
6. Log meetings in CRM
7. Total time: 1-1.5 hours/day for 30-50 new prospects

## LinkedIn Automation: Risks and Guidelines

**This section requires serious attention. LinkedIn automation carries real risk.**

### LinkedIn's Position on Automation
- LinkedIn's Terms of Service explicitly prohibit automated tools that scrape data or automate actions
- LinkedIn actively detects and restricts accounts using automation
- Penalties range from temporary restrictions to permanent bans
- Your LinkedIn profile is a long-term professional asset — losing it has consequences beyond sales

### What Gets Accounts Restricted
- Sending too many connection requests per day (>50-80 seems to trigger flags)
- Connection request acceptance rate too low (<20% — LinkedIn sees this as spam)
- Sending identical messages to many people in a short time
- Using known automation tools that LinkedIn has fingerprinted
- Unusual activity patterns (e.g., sending 200 DMs at 3 AM)
- Multiple reports from recipients marking you as spam

### Risk Levels of LinkedIn Activities

| Activity | Risk Level | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Manually viewing profiles | None | Normal LinkedIn behavior |
| Manually sending connection requests | None | Stay under 100/week |
| Manually sending DMs | None | Normal behavior |
| Using LinkedIn's own Sales Navigator features | None | It's their product |
| Using browser extensions to view profiles | Low-Medium | Some are detectable |
| Using tools to auto-send connection requests | Medium-High | Detectable, Terms violation |
| Using tools to auto-send messages | High | Major Terms violation |
| Scraping LinkedIn data at scale | Very High | Legal risk, not just account risk |

### The "Manual at Scale" Approach (Recommended)

Instead of automating LinkedIn actions, systematize the manual process:

1. **Batch your LinkedIn work.** Dedicate 30-60 minutes daily to LinkedIn outreach:
   - 15 min: Profile views and content engagement
   - 15 min: Send 15-25 connection requests with personalized notes
   - 15 min: Send DMs to new connections
   - 15 min: Follow up on existing conversations

2. **Use templates, not automation.** Keep a document with DM templates. Copy-paste and personalize the first line manually. This takes 30 seconds per message.

3. **Use Sales Navigator for targeting, not automation for sending.** Sales Navigator's advanced filters are extremely powerful for finding the right people. The sending should be manual.

4. **Track in your CRM, not in a LinkedIn tool.** Log LinkedIn activity in your CRM alongside email activity for a unified view.

### If You Choose to Automate LinkedIn (Understand the Risks)

If you accept the risks and decide to use LinkedIn automation:
- Use the most conservative settings (5-10 connection requests/day, not 50)
- Never automate messages to people you haven't connected with
- Review every message before it sends (use tools with approval workflows)
- Use randomized delays between actions
- Stop immediately if you receive any LinkedIn warning
- Have a backup plan if your account is restricted
- Never use automation on your personal LinkedIn if it's your primary professional identity — consider a secondary profile dedicated to outreach (though this also has risks)

## The Automation Stack Build: Step-by-Step

### Step 1: Start Manual (Weeks 1-4)

**Do everything by hand first.** This is not optional.

Why:
- You learn the actual workflow and identify bottlenecks
- You develop intuition for what good vs. bad looks like
- You create your first templates based on real experience
- You understand the process well enough to know WHAT to automate

What to do:
- Send 10-20 outreach emails per day, manually
- Send 10-15 LinkedIn connection requests per day, manually
- Track everything in a spreadsheet
- Note which tasks take the most time and feel the most repetitive

### Step 2: Document the Process (Week 4-5)

Create simple SOPs (Standard Operating Procedures) for each task:

**Example SOP: Daily Email Outreach**
1. Open prospect list in spreadsheet
2. Select next 20 uncontacted prospects
3. For each prospect:
   a. Check LinkedIn profile (2 min)
   b. Find a personalization angle (1 min)
   c. Write and send email using Template A (2 min)
   d. Log in spreadsheet: date sent, template used, personalization note
4. Check for replies from previous sends
5. Respond to all replies within 1 hour
6. Update spreadsheet with reply status
Total time: 90-120 minutes

**Why document before automating:**
- You can't automate what you haven't defined
- SOPs become the requirements for your automation setup
- If automation breaks, SOPs are your fallback
- SOPs make it easy to delegate to a VA or team member if you choose that route instead

### Step 3: Identify Automation Opportunities (Week 5-6)

Using the Automation Opportunity Map above, highlight every task in your SOPs that falls into "Automate Fully" or "Automate with Human Review."

For each opportunity, document:
- Current time spent per week on this task
- Potential time saved with automation
- Risk of quality degradation if automated
- Tool needed to automate

Prioritize by: (Time Saved x Frequency) / Risk

### Step 4: Choose Tools (Week 6-7)

Based on your budget and priorities:

1. **Start with the tool that saves the most time.** Usually this is an email sequencing tool.
2. **Don't buy everything at once.** Add one tool at a time.
3. **Use free trials.** Test before committing.
4. **Check integration compatibility.** Will tool A work with tool B?

**Decision framework for choosing between similar tools:**
- Read reviews from people in YOUR industry/company size
- Check the tool's subreddit or community forum for honest feedback
- Test deliverability specifically (some tools have better reputations than others)
- Evaluate the learning curve — a powerful tool you don't use is worthless
- Consider support quality — you'll need help during setup

### Step 5: Set Up and Test (Weeks 7-9)

1. **Set up the tool following their onboarding process.** Don't skip steps.
2. **Import a SMALL batch first.** 50-100 contacts, not your entire list.
3. **Send to yourself and team members first.** Check formatting, deliverability, personalization variables.
4. **Run at low volume for 1 week.** 10-20 emails/day per inbox to test.
5. **Monitor everything:** delivery rate, open rate, reply rate. Compare to your manual baseline.
6. **Fix issues before scaling.** If deliverability drops, stop and troubleshoot.

### Step 6: Monitor and Adjust (Ongoing)

Automation is not "set and forget." It requires regular maintenance:

**Daily (5 minutes):**
- Check for bounces or deliverability issues
- Review any flagged replies
- Ensure sequences are running (tools sometimes pause unexpectedly)

**Weekly (15 minutes):**
- Review campaign metrics
- Check sending reputation
- Clear any stuck contacts from sequences
- Review and respond to edge-case replies

**Monthly (30 minutes):**
- Audit all active sequences for relevance
- Update templates that are underperforming
- Review tool usage — are you using what you're paying for?
- Check for tool updates or new features

### Step 7: Scale Gradually (Week 10+)

Once your automation is running smoothly at low volume:

1. **Increase volume by 25-50% per week.** Not all at once.
2. **Add new inboxes before increasing per-inbox volume.** Better to have 5 inboxes sending 30/day than 1 inbox sending 150/day.
3. **Add new campaigns and audiences.** Test new ICP segments.
4. **Layer in additional channels.** Add LinkedIn touches to email sequences.
5. **Monitor quality metrics at each increase.** If reply rates drop as volume increases, you've scaled too fast.

## Time Savings Calculation

Use this framework to estimate ROI from automation:

| Task | Manual Time/Week | Automated Time/Week | Time Saved/Week |
|------|-----------------|--------------------|-----------------| 
| Sending outreach emails | 5-8 hours | 30 min (review + monitoring) | 4.5-7.5 hours |
| Follow-up management | 2-3 hours | 15 min (exception handling) | 1.75-2.75 hours |
| Data entry / CRM updates | 2-3 hours | 15 min (spot checks) | 1.75-2.75 hours |
| List building / research | 3-5 hours | 1-2 hours (review + enrichment) | 2-3 hours |
| Email verification | 1-2 hours | 5 min (automated batch) | 1-2 hours |
| Scheduling meetings | 1-2 hours | 15 min (scheduling link) | 0.75-1.75 hours |
| Basic reporting | 1-2 hours | 15 min (automated dashboards) | 0.75-1.75 hours |
| **TOTAL** | **15-25 hours** | **2.5-4.5 hours** | **12-20 hours** |

**ROI calculation:**
- Hours saved per week: 12-20
- Value of your time per hour: $[YOUR RATE]
- Weekly value of time saved: $[HOURS x RATE]
- Monthly tool cost: $[TOTAL TOOLS]
- Monthly ROI: (Weekly value x 4) - Monthly tool cost

If the time savings don't justify the cost, you're either automating the wrong things or using tools that are too expensive for your stage.

- [ ] All sequences are actively sending (no paused campaigns you forgot about)
- [ ] Bounce rate is below 2% across all campaigns
- [ ] No unusual deliverability drops
- [ ] Reply handling is caught up (no unanswered replies older than 24 hours)

- [ ] All tools are being used (cancel anything you're paying for but not using)

- [ ] Sending domains are healthy (check reputation)

- [ ] Integration between tools is working (CRM sync, enrichment flow)

- [ ] Full tool stack audit — is each tool still the best option?

- [ ] Update SOPs to reflect any workflow changes
- [ ] Evaluate whether any "keep manual" tasks should move to "automate with review"
- [ ] Assess team growth — do you need team-tier plans?

## Common Automation Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Over-Automating Personal Touches
**What happens:** You automate LinkedIn DMs, and prospects notice immediately. The messages feel generic, arrive at suspicious times, and use the same patterns as every other automated message.
**The fix:** Keep first-touch personalization manual. Automate only follow-ups and only when the follow-up adds genuine value.

### Mistake 2: Under-Monitoring
**What happens:** A sequence has been sending broken personalization ({first_name} appears literally) for 2 weeks before you notice. Or your domain gets blacklisted and 500 emails go to spam.
**The fix:** Check your automation daily. Set up alerts for metric drops. Send test emails to yourself weekly.

### Mistake 3: Not Updating Templates
**What happens:** You write great templates in month 1 and never update them. By month 4, they're stale — references are outdated, market conditions have changed, and your prospects have seen similar messages from competitors.
**The fix:** Refresh all templates every 60-90 days. Review performance monthly and replace any underperformers.

### Mistake 4: Tool Sprawl
**What happens:** You sign up for 8 different tools that each do one thing. They don't integrate well, data lives in silos, and you spend more time managing tools than doing outreach.
**The fix:** Consolidate. Choose platforms that handle multiple functions. Prioritize tools that integrate natively with each other. Cancel what you don't use.

### Mistake 5: Automating Before Validating
**What happens:** You set up an elaborate automation stack before confirming that your messaging actually works. You scale a broken process and burn through your prospect list.
**The fix:** Always start manual. Only automate what you've proven works by hand first.

### Mistake 6: Ignoring Compliance
**What happens:** You send emails without proper unsubscribe links, violate CAN-SPAM or GDPR, or automate LinkedIn in ways that get your account banned.
**The fix:** Understand the legal requirements for your target market. Include unsubscribe options. Respect opt-outs immediately. Use tools that handle compliance natively.
