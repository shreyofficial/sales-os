# Monthly Sales OS Health Check

## Purpose

Outreach performance degrades over time. Templates get stale. Market conditions shift. Competitors adapt. Your ICP evolves. Channels change their algorithms. What worked 3 months ago may be actively hurting you today. This monthly health check is a comprehensive audit across every component of your sales operating system — designed to catch decay early, identify what's working so you can double down, and keep the entire machine running at peak performance.

---

## Why Monthly Health Checks Matter

### The Performance Decay Curve

Every outreach campaign follows a predictable decay curve:

```
Performance
    ^
    |   /‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾\
    |  /             \
    | /               \‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾  ← Without health checks
    |/                 \            (gradual decay to mediocrity)
    |                   \
    |  /‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾  ← With monthly refresh
    | /                              (sustained performance)
    +————————————————————————————→ Time
    Month 1   Month 2   Month 3   Month 4
```

**Why performance decays:**
- **Template fatigue:** Your ICP starts seeing your messaging pattern (especially in niche markets)
- **Market saturation:** Competitors copy successful approaches, raising the bar for standing out
- **Data decay:** Contact data degrades at ~30% per year (job changes, company changes, email changes)
- **Algorithm changes:** Email providers and LinkedIn update their spam/content filters
- **Seasonal shifts:** Buyer behavior changes throughout the year
- **Your own evolution:** Your offer, positioning, and ideal client may have shifted

### The Compounding Cost of Neglect

Letting performance decay unchecked compounds:
- Month 1: Reply rate drops 1% — barely noticeable
- Month 2: Another 1% drop — "probably just noise"
- Month 3: Another 1-2% drop — "maybe the market is slow"
- Month 4: You've lost 30-40% of your peak performance and burned through a significant chunk of your addressable market with underperforming messaging

Monthly health checks catch the 1% drops before they compound into major problems.

## The Monthly Health Check Framework

Block 30-45 minutes on your calendar on the same day each month. Treat this as non-negotiable — it's the maintenance that keeps the machine running.

---

### Section 1: Foundation Check (5 minutes)

This section audits the fundamentals that everything else is built on.

#### ICP Accuracy Check

**Questions to answer:**
- In the last month, who were your best conversations/meetings with? Did they match your defined ICP?
- Were there any surprise wins from outside your ICP? (Signal to expand or shift targeting)
- Were there any ICP matches that turned out to be poor fits? (Signal to narrow targeting)
- Has anything changed about your ICP's typical pain points, buying process, or decision-making timeline?

**Action items:**
- [ ] Review last month's meetings: Do they match ICP? Note any outliers.
- [ ] Check: Are the job titles, company sizes, and industries in your targeting still accurate?
- [ ] Update ICP document if any changes are identified.
- [ ] Status: [NO CHANGES NEEDED / MINOR UPDATE / MAJOR REVISION NEEDED]

#### Offer & Positioning Check

**Questions to answer:**
- Has your product, service, or pricing changed in the last month?
- Have you received any consistent feedback that suggests your positioning is off?
- Is there a new angle, feature, or result that should be highlighted in outreach?
- Has a competitor launched something that changes how you need to position?

**Action items:**
- [ ] Review any product/service changes from the last month
- [ ] Check recent prospect feedback for positioning insights
- [ ] Update value proposition language if needed
- [ ] Status: [NO CHANGES NEEDED / MINOR UPDATE / MAJOR REVISION NEEDED]

#### Competitive Landscape Check

**Questions to answer:**
- Are prospects mentioning any new competitors?
- Have existing competitors changed their messaging, pricing, or approach?
- Is anyone else targeting your exact ICP with a similar offer?
- Are you winning or losing more competitive deals than last month?

**Action items:**
- [ ] Quick scan of 2-3 competitor LinkedIn profiles and websites
- [ ] Note any new competitors mentioned by prospects
- [ ] Update competitive differentiation points if needed
- [ ] Status: [NO CHANGES / NEW COMPETITOR / POSITIONING SHIFT NEEDED]

#### New Sales Assets Check

**Questions to answer:**
- Any new case studies or testimonials from the last month?
- Any new results or metrics you can reference?
- Any new content (posts, articles, talks) that adds credibility?
- Any new partnerships or credentials?

**Action items:**
- [ ] Collect any new testimonials or results data
- [ ] Create 1-2 sentence case study snippets for outreach use
- [ ] Update the social proof library
- [ ] Status: [NO NEW ASSETS / NEW ASSETS ADDED TO LIBRARY]

### Section 5: Cross-Channel Check (5 minutes)

Assess how well your channels work together.

#### Omnichannel Cadence Health

**Questions to answer:**
- Is the multi-channel sequence running smoothly (email + LinkedIn + any other channels)?
- Are prospects receiving touches in the right order across channels?
- Any channel that's significantly outperforming or underperforming the others?
- Are there prospects stuck between channels (e.g., connected on LinkedIn but never emailed)?

**Action items:**
- [ ] Verify cross-channel sequences are triggering correctly
- [ ] Check for orphaned prospects (started on one channel, never contacted on others)
- [ ] Review channel attribution — which channel drives the most meetings?
- [ ] Status: [RUNNING SMOOTHLY / NEEDS ADJUSTMENT / NOT YET IMPLEMENTED]

#### Lead Scoring Accuracy

**Questions to answer:**
- Are high-scored leads actually converting to meetings?
- Are low-scored leads surprising you with positive responses?
- Does the scoring model need recalibration based on actual data?

**Action items:**
- [ ] Compare lead scores to actual conversion data from last month
- [ ] Note any mismatches (high scores that didn't convert, low scores that did)
- [ ] Adjust scoring criteria if significant mismatches exist
- [ ] Status: [ACCURATE / NEEDS RECALIBRATION / NOT YET IMPLEMENTED]

#### Pipeline Shape

A healthy pipeline has a funnel shape — more prospects at the top, fewer at each subsequent stage.

| Stage | Count | % of Total | Healthy Range |
|-------|-------|-----------|---------------|
| Prospects identified | | | 100% |
| Contacted (first touch sent) | | | 70-90% |
| Engaged (opened/clicked/viewed) | | | 30-50% |
| Replied (any reply) | | | 5-15% |
| Positive reply | | | 2-5% |
| Meeting booked | | | 1-3% |
| Meeting completed | | | 0.5-2% |

**Warning signs:**
- Bottleneck at "Contacted" → not enough outreach volume
- Bottleneck at "Engaged" → deliverability or targeting problem
- Bottleneck at "Replied" → messaging or CTA problem
- Bottleneck at "Meeting booked" → conversion process problem
- Bottleneck at "Meeting completed" → no-show problem

**Action items:**
- [ ] Map current pipeline by stage
- [ ] Identify any new bottlenecks vs. last month
- [ ] Flag any stage that's significantly worse than last month
- [ ] Status: [HEALTHY FUNNEL SHAPE / BOTTLENECK AT (STAGE)]

## The "Refresh or Retire" Decision

For every component of your Sales OS, make one of three decisions each month:

### KEEP (No changes needed)
- Performance is stable or improving
- No new information that changes the approach
- Still aligned with current offer and ICP
- Note: Even "keep" decisions should be explicitly documented, not assumed by default

### REFRESH (Update and improve)
- Performance has declined but the core approach is sound
- New data available (case studies, results, market shifts) that should be incorporated
- Template has been running for 3+ months and needs updated language
- Minor targeting adjustments needed

### RETIRE (Replace entirely)
- Performance is consistently below benchmark despite optimization attempts
- Fundamental approach is no longer aligned with business direction
- Market has shifted enough that the entire angle is outdated
- Template has been sent to most of the addressable market

**Apply this decision to:**
- [ ] Each email sequence
- [ ] Each LinkedIn DM template
- [ ] Each connection request note
- [ ] The ICP definition
- [ ] The value proposition
- [ ] The competitive positioning
- [ ] The lead scoring model
- [ ] The automation workflow

## The "One Big Win" and "One Big Fix" Framework

Each monthly review should produce exactly two outputs:

### One Big Win
**What single thing is working best right now that we should double down on?**

This could be:
- A specific email template with above-average reply rate
- A LinkedIn content strategy that's generating inbound
- A particular ICP segment that's converting at high rates
- A specific automation that's saving significant time
- A new approach (voice notes, video, multi-channel) that's outperforming

**Action:** Allocate more resources to this. If a template is winning, scale it. If an ICP segment converts well, send more volume there. If a content strategy works, post more frequently.

Document: "This month's Big Win: [DESCRIPTION]. Plan to double down: [SPECIFIC ACTIONS]."

### One Big Fix
**What single thing is the biggest bottleneck or problem that needs to be fixed?**

This could be:
- A deliverability issue dragging down all campaigns
- A particular sequence with declining performance
- A channel that's underperforming expectations
- A process bottleneck (e.g., slow response to positive replies)
- A tool that's not working properly

**Action:** Make fixing this the #1 priority for the coming month. Define specific steps, assign a timeline, and set success criteria.

Document: "This month's Big Fix: [DESCRIPTION]. Fix plan: [SPECIFIC ACTIONS]. Success criteria: [METRIC TARGET]. Timeline: [DEADLINE]."

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing the health check report, verify:

- [ ] Every section has been reviewed — no section skipped, even if "no changes" (that should be noted explicitly)
- [ ] All metrics are current and accurately calculated
- [ ] Month-over-month trends are tracked for all key metrics (at least 3 months when available)
- [ ] The "One Big Win" is identified and a scale-up plan is defined
- [ ] The "One Big Fix" is identified and a fix plan with timeline is defined
- [ ] Every active campaign/template has a Keep/Refresh/Retire decision
- [ ] Pipeline targets are compared to actual performance
- [ ] Competitive landscape has been briefly reviewed
- [ ] All tools and automations have been checked for proper operation
- [ ] Deliverability has been specifically audited (not just assumed healthy)
- [ ] LinkedIn and email channels are both covered (not just one)
- [ ] Cross-channel coordination has been evaluated
- [ ] The action plan for next month is prioritized and specific (not vague)
- [ ] A date for next month's health check is scheduled
- [ ] New sales assets (case studies, testimonials) have been captured and incorporated
- [ ] The report is personalized to the user's actual data and situation
- [ ] Foundation check items (ICP, offer, competition) are covered even if unchanged — explicitly noting "no changes" prevents blind spots
