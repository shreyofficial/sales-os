# Sales Asset Auditor

## Purpose

This skill takes inventory of everything the user currently has that can support sales outreach — then scores it, identifies gaps, and creates a prioritized plan for what to build next. Most businesses have more assets than they realize, but they are scattered, outdated, or not optimized for outreach. Conversely, most businesses are missing 2-3 critical assets that would dramatically improve their conversion rates.

The output is a Sales Asset Inventory with scores, a gap analysis, and a prioritized build list that feeds directly into the content and campaign skills that follow.

---

## How This Skill Works

### Step 1: Collect the Inventory

Walk the user through every category of sales asset. For each, determine: Does it exist? What condition is it in? Is it usable for outreach?

Do NOT ask about all categories at once. Group them logically and ask in batches.

#### Batch 1: Digital Presence (Ask First)

**1. Website / Landing Pages**
- Do you have a website? Is it current?
- Do you have dedicated landing pages for specific offers or audiences?
- Is there a clear call-to-action? What is it? (Book a demo, free trial, contact us, etc.)
- Does the website clearly communicate what you do, who you do it for, and why you are different?

**2. LinkedIn Presence**
- Is your personal LinkedIn profile optimized for your current role and offer?
- Do you have a company LinkedIn page? Is it active?
- Are you posting content regularly? What type? How often?
- What does your LinkedIn headline say? Does it speak to the ICP's problem?
- What does your About section say?

**3. Other Social Presence**
- Do you have a presence on Twitter/X, YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, or other platforms?
- Which platforms are active vs. dormant?
- Which platforms are relevant to your ICP?

#### Batch 2: Proof Assets (Ask Second)

**4. Case Studies**
- Do you have formal written case studies?
- If yes: How many? What industries/use cases do they cover? How old are they?
- If no: Do you have client results you COULD turn into case studies?

**5. Testimonials**
- Do you have written testimonials from clients?
- Do you have video testimonials?
- Are these on your website? On LinkedIn? Somewhere else?
- Do they include the client's name, title, and company? (Named testimonials are 3-5x more powerful than anonymous ones.)

**6. Social Proof**
- Do you have notable client logos you can reference?
- Do you have aggregate metrics? ("500+ companies," "10,000+ users," "$50M in pipeline generated")
- Do you have review site ratings? (G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, Google Reviews)
- Do you have press mentions, awards, or certifications?

**7. Results and Data**
- Do you have specific, quantified results for clients? (e.g., "Increased revenue by 40%," "Reduced churn by 25%")
- Do you have before/after comparisons?
- Do you have ROI calculations or projections?

#### Batch 3: Sales Collateral (Ask Third)

**8. Pitch Deck / Sales Deck**
- Do you have a pitch deck or sales presentation?
- When was it last updated?
- Does it follow the positioning from the Offer Positioning Engine output?
- How many slides? (Ideal for outreach support: 8-12 slides)

**9. One-Pagers / Leave-Behinds**
- Do you have a one-page overview of your product/service?
- Is it designed for prospects to share internally with their team?
- Does it have a clear call-to-action?

**10. Pricing Documentation**
- Do you have a pricing page or document?
- Is pricing transparent or "contact us"?
- Do you have different pricing tiers or packages clearly laid out?

**11. Comparison / Battle Cards**
- Do you have a document that compares you to specific competitors?
- Is it designed for the prospect or for internal sales use?
- Is it factual and fair, or does it feel biased?

**12. Proposals / SOWs**
- Do you have a standard proposal template?
- Is it professional and branded?
- Can it be customized quickly for new prospects?

#### Batch 4: Content Assets (Ask Fourth)

**13. Blog Posts / Articles**
- Do you have a blog? How many posts?
- What topics do they cover?
- Are any posts directly relevant to the ICP's pain points?
- What is the traffic/engagement like?

**14. Lead Magnets**
- Do you have any downloadable resources? (Guides, templates, checklists, calculators, assessments)
- What is the topic? Is it aligned with the ICP's pain points?
- Is there a landing page and email capture for it?

**15. Webinars / Workshops**
- Have you hosted webinars or workshops?
- Are the recordings available?
- What topics did they cover?

**16. Video Content**
- Do you have a demo video or product walkthrough?
- Do you have explainer videos?
- Do you have thought leadership video content?
- Where is it hosted? (YouTube, Loom, Vimeo, website)

**17. Podcasts / Interviews**
- Have you been on podcasts? Do you host one?
- Are there specific episodes relevant to the ICP?

**18. Whitepapers / Research**
- Do you have any original research, whitepapers, or industry reports?
- Are they gated (email required) or public?

#### Batch 5: Outreach-Specific Assets (Ask Last)

**19. Email Templates**
- Do you have cold email templates you are currently using?
- Do you have follow-up email sequences?
- How many touchpoints in your sequence?
- What is your current response rate? (If known)

**20. LinkedIn DM Scripts**
- Do you have LinkedIn connection request templates?
- Do you have follow-up DM sequences?
- What is your current acceptance/response rate? (If known)

**21. Call Scripts**
- Do you have a cold call or discovery call script?
- Do you have a demo script?
- Do you have objection-handling cheat sheets?

**22. Voicemail / Video Message Scripts**
- Do you leave voicemails as part of outreach?
- Do you send personalized video messages? (Loom, Vidyard)
- Do you have templates for these?

### Step 2: Score Each Asset

For every asset that exists, assign a **Sales-Ready Score** from 1-5:

#### The Scoring Framework

**Score 5 — Outreach Weapon**
- Directly relevant to the ICP's pain points
- Includes specific, quantified results or proof
- Professionally produced (good design, clear copy, no errors)
- Current and up-to-date (within the last 6 months)
- Can be shared in outreach with zero modification
- Example: A case study featuring a Tier 1 ICP company with a named quote and 3 specific metrics

**Score 4 — Strong Asset**
- Relevant to the ICP but could be more specific
- Has proof elements but could be stronger
- Well-produced with minor room for improvement
- Mostly current
- Can be used in outreach with minor framing
- Example: A polished pitch deck that covers the right points but was designed for investors, not prospects

**Score 3 — Usable But Needs Work**
- Somewhat relevant but too general
- Has some proof but it is vague or dated
- Acceptable production quality
- Needs updating or customization to be outreach-ready
- Example: A blog post about the right topic but written 18 months ago without specific client data

**Score 2 — Weak / Outdated**
- Tangentially relevant
- No proof or social proof elements
- Production quality is below standard
- Outdated information or positioning
- Requires significant rework to be usable
- Example: A testimonial from 3 years ago from a client in an industry you no longer target

**Score 1 — Exists But Not Usable**
- Not relevant to current ICP or positioning
- No proof elements
- Poor quality
- Badly outdated
- Would need to be rebuilt from scratch
- Example: A company overview PDF from when the product was something completely different

**Score 0 — Does Not Exist**
- The asset does not exist at all in any form.

### Step 3: Gap Analysis

After scoring everything, identify the critical gaps.

#### The "Must-Have" Asset Stack for Outreach

These are the assets that have the highest impact on outreach conversion rates. If they do not exist or score below 3, they are critical gaps.

**Tier A — Cannot Run Effective Outreach Without These**

1. **Optimized LinkedIn Profile (Score 4+)** — This is the first thing every prospect checks after receiving your DM. If it does not clearly communicate your value, every message is wasted.

2. **At Least 1 Strong Case Study or Client Result (Score 4+)** — Outreach without proof is just noise. You need at least one concrete example of a result you have delivered.

3. **Clear One-Liner (Score 4+)** — From the Offer Positioning Engine. If you cannot explain what you do in one sentence, no outreach message will be compelling.

4. **A Website That Does Not Embarrass You (Score 3+)** — It does not need to be world-class, but it needs to confirm your legitimacy when a prospect checks.

**Tier B — Significantly Improves Response Rates**

5. **2-3 Additional Proof Points (Score 3+)** — Different industries, use cases, or result types. More proof = more relevance for more prospects.

6. **A Lead Magnet or Free Resource (Score 3+)** — Gives you a reason to reach out beyond "Can I have 15 minutes?" Providing value upfront dramatically improves response rates.

7. **A One-Pager (Score 3+)** — Something the prospect can share with their team. Decisions are rarely made by one person — give your champion a tool to sell internally.

8. **A Demo Video or Product Walkthrough (Score 3+)** — Lets the prospect experience your solution without committing to a call. Reduces friction in the funnel.

**Tier C — Nice to Have, Builds Over Time**

9. **Competitive Comparison Sheet (Score 3+)** — Helps when prospects are evaluating alternatives.
10. **Active LinkedIn Content (Regular posting)** — Builds credibility over time and warms up prospects before outreach.
11. **ROI Calculator or Assessment Tool** — Makes the value tangible and personalized.
12. **Webinar or Workshop Recording** — Deep-dive content for engaged prospects.
13. **Multiple Case Studies Across Verticals (Score 4+)** — One per target industry.

### Step 4: Priority Matrix

Score each missing or low-scoring asset on two dimensions:

**Impact on Outreach Effectiveness (1-5)**
- How much would this asset improve response rates, booking rates, or close rates?
- 5 = Directly addresses the #1 reason prospects ignore or reject outreach.
- 1 = Marginal improvement, mostly cosmetic.

**Effort to Create (1-5, inverted: 5 = easiest)**
- How long would it take to create a usable version?
- 5 = Can be created in under 1 hour with existing information.
- 1 = Requires weeks of work, external resources, or significant investment.

**Priority Score = Impact x Effort**

| Asset | Impact (1-5) | Effort (5=easy) | Priority Score | Priority Rank |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|----------------|---------------|
| [Asset] | [Score] | [Score] | [Product] | [Rank] |

Sort by Priority Score descending. The top 3-5 items become the immediate build list.

### Step 5: Quick-Build Guides for High-Priority Missing Assets

For each high-priority missing asset, provide a quick-build guide:

#### Quick-Build: LinkedIn Profile Optimization
- **Time required:** 30-60 minutes
- **Process:**
  1. Rewrite headline using the formula: "[Role] | I help [ICP] achieve [Outcome] | [Proof point]"
  2. Rewrite About section: Problem you solve (paragraph 1), how you solve it (paragraph 2), proof it works (paragraph 3), call to action (paragraph 4)
  3. Update Experience section to focus on results delivered, not job descriptions
  4. Add relevant skills, request endorsements
  5. Update banner image to reflect current positioning
  6. Ensure profile photo is professional and approachable

#### Quick-Build: Case Study (From Existing Client)
- **Time required:** 1-2 hours
- **Process:**
  1. Pick your best client result
  2. Structure: Situation (who they are, what they were struggling with), Challenge (the specific problem), Solution (what you did), Results (quantified outcomes), Quote (client testimonial)
  3. Keep it to 1 page or 500-800 words
  4. Include at least 2-3 specific numbers
  5. Get client approval to use their name (or anonymize if needed)
  6. Format as PDF and also as a LinkedIn post

#### Quick-Build: One-Pager
- **Time required:** 1-2 hours
- **Process:**
  1. One page, front only
  2. Top: Headline that states the transformation
  3. Middle left: 3 key benefits (outcome-focused, not feature-focused)
  4. Middle right: Social proof (logo bar, key metric, testimonial quote)
  5. Bottom: Call to action with contact info
  6. Design does not need to be fancy — clean and professional is enough

#### Quick-Build: Lead Magnet
- **Time required:** 2-4 hours
- **Process:**
  1. Pick the #1 pain point from the ICP Document
  2. Create a resource that helps the prospect make progress on that pain (checklist, template, guide, framework)
  3. Keep it short and immediately useful — 3-7 pages maximum
  4. Title formula: "The [ICP Role]'s Guide to [Desired Outcome]" or "[Number] [Framework/Steps/Mistakes] for [Desired Outcome]"
  5. Include your branding and a soft CTA at the end
  6. Host behind a simple landing page with email capture

#### Quick-Build: Demo / Walkthrough Video
- **Time required:** 1-2 hours
- **Process:**
  1. Use Loom or similar screen recording tool
  2. Structure: Hook (state the problem in 10 seconds), Overview (what the solution does in 30 seconds), Walkthrough (show it working for 2-4 minutes), Result (what they will achieve), CTA (next step)
  3. Keep it under 5 minutes total
  4. Do not try to show everything — show the 2-3 most compelling features
  5. Speak to the ICP's pain, not your features

#### Quick-Build: Testimonial Collection
- **Time required:** 30 minutes (plus waiting for responses)
- **Process:**
  1. Email 5-10 happy clients with this template:
     "Hi [Name], I am putting together some materials for our website and would love to include your experience. Would you mind answering 2-3 quick questions?
     - What was the main challenge you were facing before working with us?
     - What specific results have you seen since working with us?
     - What would you say to someone considering working with us?
     No need to write a novel — a few sentences for each is perfect."
  2. Follow up once if no response after 5 days
  3. Get permission to use their name and company
  4. Format best responses as quotation cards for website and outreach

#### Quick-Build: Competitive Comparison Sheet
- **Time required:** 1-2 hours
- **Process:**
  1. Pull from the Competitive Intelligence Brief
  2. Create a simple comparison table: You vs. Top 2-3 alternatives
  3. Choose 5-7 comparison criteria where you are strong
  4. Be honest — mark areas where competitors are also strong
  5. Include a "best for" summary at the bottom
  6. Keep it factual, not disparaging

## Quality Checklist

Before marking this skill complete, verify:

- [ ] **Every asset category has been evaluated** — Nothing was skipped or assumed.
- [ ] **Scores are honest and justified** — Not inflated to make the user feel good. A 2 is a 2.
- [ ] **Gaps are prioritized by impact, not completeness** — The goal is not to have everything; it is to have the right things.
- [ ] **Quick-build guides are actionable** — The user can follow them without additional research.
- [ ] **Asset usage map connects assets to outreach stages** — Every usable asset has a clear deployment point.
- [ ] **The user has confirmed the inventory** — They have not forgotten any assets and agree with the scores.
- [ ] **Critical gaps have a timeline** — The user knows what to build and by when.
- [ ] **Proof assets are specifically evaluated** — Case studies, testimonials, and results are scrutinized — these have the highest outreach impact.
- [ ] **LinkedIn profile has been specifically assessed** — This is the #1 asset for LinkedIn outreach and deserves dedicated attention.
- [ ] **Existing outreach materials are preserved** — If they have templates that are working, do not suggest replacing them. Build on what works.

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Overwhelming the user with gaps.** Every business has gaps. Focus on the 3-5 that will move the needle most. The rest can wait.

2. **Overvaluing polished assets.** A rough case study with real numbers beats a beautifully designed one-pager with no proof. Score based on outreach effectiveness, not aesthetics.

3. **Ignoring existing outreach data.** If the user has been doing outreach and has response rate data, that is gold. An email template with a 5% response rate is more valuable than a new one that is untested.

4. **Treating all assets as equal.** A case study is worth 10 blog posts for outreach purposes. A strong testimonial is worth more than a polished website. Weight the scoring accordingly.

5. **Not connecting assets to outreach stages.** An asset that exists but is never used in outreach is the same as one that does not exist. The usage map is not optional.

6. **Suggesting the user create everything at once.** That leads to paralysis. Give them a focused 1-week sprint: "Build these 3 things in this order. Everything else can wait."

## Handoff

When this skill is complete, the Sales Asset Inventory is saved and made available to:
- **All outreach skills** — The usage map determines which assets are deployed at which stage of outreach sequences.
- **Content skills** — The gap analysis and priority build list inform what content to create first.
- **Campaign skills** — Asset availability determines which campaign types are viable immediately vs. which need prep work first.
- **The user's action plan** — The priority build list becomes their immediate homework before outreach launches.
